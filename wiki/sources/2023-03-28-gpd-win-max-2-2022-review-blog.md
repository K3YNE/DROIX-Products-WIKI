## Summary

GPDStore blog review of the [[gpd-win-max-2]] AMD model (2022) powered by AMD Ryzen 7 6800U with Radeon 680M graphics.

## Key Specs

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen 7 6800U, 8 cores/16 threads, up to 4.7 GHz, 28 W TDP
- **GPU:** AMD Radeon 680M, 12 cores, up to 2200 MHz
- **Display:** 10.1" touchscreen, 1920x1200 (supports up to 2560x1600)
- **RAM options:** 16 GB / 32 GB / 64 GB LPDDR4x @ 4266 MHz
- **Storage options:** 512 GB / 1 TB / 2 TB PCIe 4.0 NVMe SSD + M.2 2230 expansion slot
- **Battery:** 57 Wh, 8-10 hours light use, 3-4 hours gaming, PD fast charge 0-50% in 30 min
- **Connectivity:** WiFi 6, Bluetooth 5.1
- **Weight:** 1005 g
- **Dimensions:** 8.9 x 6.2 x 0.9 inches (22.7 x 16 x 2.3 cm)
- **I/O:** 3.5 mm headphone, USB 3.2, HDMI, 2x USB Type-C (one USB 4), Micro/SD card slots, 2x USB 3.2 Gen 2
- **Camera:** 2 MP high refresh rate
- **Controls:** Dual clickable hall sensor analog sticks, D-pad, gaming buttons (hidden under covers)

## Emulation Performance

- NES through N64/Dreamcast: runs with ease
- GameCube/Wii (Dolphin): Super Mario Sunshine, Wind Waker, Metroid Prime at 60 FPS
- PS2 (PCSX2): God of War, Shadow of the Colossus, Gran Turismo 4 at full speed with enhancements
- Xbox 360 (Xenia) / PS3 (RPCS3): mixed results, some games 30+ FPS, others struggle

## Gaming Performance

- Fortnite, Apex Legends, Overwatch: playable at low-to-medium settings ~60 FPS
- Cyberpunk 2077, Red Dead Redemption 2, Control: playable at low settings, 30-40 FPS

## Verdict

Powerful and versatile handheld gaming device. Excellent for retro emulation through PS2/GameCube era. Capable of modern AAA games at lower settings. Strong productivity device with full Windows 11.
