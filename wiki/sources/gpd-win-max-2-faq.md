## Frequently Asked Questions

### GPD WIN MAX 2

<details>
<summary>What are the key features of the GPD WIN MAX 2’s built-in gaming controller?</summary>
- Hall sensor analog sticks: The device uses Hall sensor sticks with built-in inductance coils instead of traditional potentiometer sticks. This eliminates issues like stick drift and wear and tear.
- Removable magnetic covers: The gaming controls can be covered by magnetic panels when not in use, allowing the device to transition between gaming and professional modes.
- Analog triggers: The triggers support 256 levels of feedback, allowing for more immersive experiences in racing games and shooters.
- Dual vibration motors: Built-in vibration motors provide haptic feedback, with adjustable intensity levels (off, weak, strong).
- 6-axis gyroscope: Supports motion control for compatible games.
- Mappable back buttons: Two customizable buttons on the rear of the device can be mapped to different functions.
- Standard gaming buttons: It includes a D-pad and standard gaming face buttons.
- Integrated design: The controls are built into the lower half of the device, allowing it to function as both a handheld gaming system and a laptop.

These features combine to provide a versatile and high-quality gaming experience on a portable Windows device.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I connect external peripherals to the GPD WIN MAX 2?</summary>
Yes, the device offers a range of ports including USB Type-A, USB-C, and Thunderbolt 4/USB4, allowing you to connect various peripherals like keyboards, mice, and external storage devices.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use the GPD WIN MAX 2 for work as well as gaming?</summary>
Absolutely. The GPD WIN MAX 2 functions as a full Windows PC with a built-in keyboard and touchpad. It can run productivity software and comes with magnetic covers for the gaming controls to give it a more professional appearance when needed.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What operating system does the GPD WIN MAX 2 run?</summary>
The GPD WIN MAX 2 comes pre-installed with a licensed Windows 11 Home edition. This provides a familiar interface and compatibility with a wide range of PC games and software.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Does the GPD WIN MAX 2 support stylus input?</summary>
Yes, the GPD WIN MAX 2 supports stylus input with an optional active pen that offers 4096 levels of pressure sensitivity. We recommend the [GPD Stylus](https://gpdstore.net/shop/accessories/gpd-stylus/) which works great with it.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use the GPD WIN MAX 2 as a regular laptop?</summary>
Yes, the GPD WIN MAX 2 functions as a fully-featured Windows laptop with a built-in keyboard and touchpad. It also includes magnetic covers for the gaming controls to give it a more professional appearance when needed.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I upgrade the storage on the GPD WIN MAX 2?</summary>
Yes, the GPD WIN MAX 2 features one M.2 2280 slot (occupied by the pre-installed SSD) and one additional M.2 2230 slot for storage expansion. Both slots support PCIe 4.0 x4 SSDs.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Does the GPD WIN MAX 2 support external GPUs?</summary>
Yes, the GPD WIN MAX 2 supports external GPUs through its USB4 port (AMD models) or Thunderbolt 4 port (Intel models), allowing for up to 40Gbps bandwidth. The [GPD WIN MAX 2 2024](https://gpdstore.net/shop/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-max-2-2024/) also has an OCuLink port compatible with the [GPD G1](https://gpdstore.net/shop/accessories/gpd-g1/) for faster data transfer speeds and performance.
</details>

<details>

<summary>Does the GPD WIN MAX 2 support external displays?</summary>
The GPD WIN MAX 2 series has a HDMI port and a USB-C port that you can use with a hub for HDMI output to a TV or monitor. See [our accessories](https://gpdstore.net/category/accessories/) for compatible hubs.

<details>
<summary>Is the GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 battery better than other 2024 models?</summary>
The GPD WIN MAX 2 2023 and 2024 have longer battery life than other devices such as the Win 4 and Mini. The Win MAX 2 is a larger device that has a larger capacity battery. For example, running Cinebench benchmark on a loop at 28W TDP got around 1 hour 52 minutes, compared to 1 hour 25 minutes on the Win 4.
</details>
