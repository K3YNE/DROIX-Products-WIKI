# AYANEO Pocket DS Review

Review published 2025-11-21 covering the [[ayaneo|AYANEO]] Pocket DS, a dual screen Android gaming handheld powered by the **Snapdragon G3X Gen 2** chipset.


## Key Specifications

- **SoC**: **Snapdragon G3X Gen 2**
- **Top Display**: 7" OLED, 1080p, up to 165Hz
- **Bottom Display**: 5" LCD, 1024x768, 60Hz
- **Weight**: 540g
- **Hinge**: Stiff, opens up to 180°
- **Colors**: Three available

## Performance and Emulation

Benchmark scores beat the [[ayaneo-pocket-ace|AYANEO Pocket ACE]] and **AYANEO Pocket DMG** despite sharing the same **Snapdragon G3X Gen 2** CPU. Dual screen emulators including Drastic, AHA, and Simu all work but require setup and configuration. The device is well suited for emulation, particularly DS-style dual screen titles.

## Design and Build

At 540g, the Pocket DS is larger and heavier than the [[ayn-thor|AYN Thor]], making it less portable. The stiff hinge allows the device to open up to 180° and holds its position well. Available in three color options.

## Comparisons

Extensively compared with the [[ayn-thor|AYN Thor]] throughout the review. Both devices have their own pros and cons, with the verdict coming down to "personal preference" rather than a clear winner. Reddit discussion echoed this sentiment with no consensus winner between the two. The Pocket DS is the larger and heavier option, while the Thor offers better portability.

## Availability

Available at [[droix|DROIX]] at the time of review.
