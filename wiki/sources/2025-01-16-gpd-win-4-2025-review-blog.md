## Summary

GPDStore blog review of the [[gpd-win-4]] 2025 with AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370 and Radeon 890M. Comprehensive benchmarks against all previous WIN 4 generations (6800U through 8840U) and competing HX 370 devices including ONEXFLY F1 Pro.

## Key Specs

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370, 12 cores/14 threads, up to 5.1 GHz
- **GPU:** AMD Radeon 890M, up to 2900 MHz
- **RAM:** 32 GB LPDDR5x @ 7500 MT/s
- **Storage:** Up to 4 TB m.2 PCIe 4.0 NVMe
- **Display:** 6" H-IPS touchscreen, 1920x1080, 40/60 Hz, 368 PPI
- **Battery:** 44.24 Wh (Cinebench loop: 57 min at 28 W; typical: 3-6 hours)
- **I/O:** USB4, USB 3.2 Gen 2, Oculink, 3.5 mm
- **Connectivity:** WiFi 6E, Bluetooth 5.3
- **Weight:** 598 g
- **Price:** $907.95-$1,284.95

## Thermals

- Fan noise: 64 dB peak
- Temperature: 45°C max

## Emulation Performance

- Effortless up to PS3 era for compatible games
- Enhanced visuals possible (higher rendering resolution, filters)
- Switch emulation (Ryujinx/Yuzu): improved over previous gen, many less demanding titles run well
- PS Vita (Vita3K): remarkable performance at increased rendering resolutions

## eGPU Performance

With [[gpd-g1|GPD G1]] or ONEXGPU 2 via Oculink: up to 128 FPS in Cyberpunk at 1080p.

## Verdict

Stellar evolution. 6800U owners: upgrade strongly recommended (+36% benchmark gains). 7840U/8840U owners: nuanced but favorable. New buyers: excellent starting point. Price: $907.95-$1,284.95.
