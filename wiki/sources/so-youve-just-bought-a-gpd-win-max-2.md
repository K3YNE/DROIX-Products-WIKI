## Checking your Hardware

1. **Test All Keyboard Keys:** Make sure all keys are responsive and registering properly.
2. **Inspect Keycaps:** Look for any cracks or damage in the keycaps. Even minor cracks can compromise the durability of the keys.
3. **Look for Dead Pixels:** Dead pixels are points on the screen that are always off. They can be noticeable and annoying, so check your device’s screen for any dead pixels.
4. **Touchscreen Deadzones:** Test every part of the touchscreen to ensure it responds correctly to touch. Make sure you can touch and interact with all corners and areas of the screen without any unresponsive zones.
5. **Check Touch Pad Functionality:** Ensure that the touchpad is functioning correctly. Any erratic or unexpected behaviour could suggest a need for a firmware update from GPD.
6. **Test SD and Micro SD Card Reading:** Test both slots with different SD and Micro SD cards to ensure they’re working correctly.
7. **Test Analog Deadzones:** The dead zone is an area where a controller’s analog stick does not respond to movement. Test your GPD device’s analog sticks to make sure they respond accurately. If you find dead zones, a firmware update might solve the issue.
8. **Test Analog Stick Circularity:** Check that the analog sticks move smoothly in all directions, ensuring full circular motion without any hitches.
9. **Test D-Pad Functionality:** The D-Pad is vital for navigation and gaming. Ensure it is responsive and functioning as expected.

## Stress Testing your GPD WIN Max 2

Once you’re sure your hardware is all good to go we can get to pushing the device as far as it goes, don’t worry it’s made to withstand high loads.

1. **Test the CPU & GPU Under Load:** Run intensive applications like high-end games and stress-testing applications such as [3D Mark](https://www.3dmark.com/), [Cinebench](https://www.maxon.net/en/cinebench), [Blender](https://opendata.blender.org/), etc., to stress the CPU and GPU. This test will reveal any possible issues with the device, such as sudden black screens during heavy usage, which could indicate a problem with the device’s hardware.
2. **Check BIOS Version:** BIOS, or Basic Input/Output System, is a crucial part of your device. You should verify if your BIOS is up-to-date. Updates can often fix issues and improve performance. However, don’t update if it isn’t necessary, as an incorrect BIOS update can cause serious problems. If you do need to update your BIOS we have a guide on that [here](https://droix.net/knowledge-base/article/how-to-install-the-gpd-win-max-2-bios-update/).
3. **Scan for Malware:** Run a full scan using a trusted antivirus tool like Microsoft Defender to detect any potential malware. Alternatively, you could reinstall the operating system for a clean start. If you do need to reinstall Windows follow our [super easy guide](https://droix.net/knowledge-base/article/how-to-reinstall-windows-on-gpd-win-devices/) to do so.

These checks are meant to ensure your WIN Max 2 works as intended and help detect any potential issues early on. If you encounter any problems, reach out to DROIX support or our community for help.
