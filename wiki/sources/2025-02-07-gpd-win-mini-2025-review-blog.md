## Summary

GPDStore blog review of the [[gpd-win-mini]] 2025 with AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370, compared against other HX 370 handhelds.

## Key Specs

- **CPU:** AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 370, 12 cores/14 threads, up to 5.1 GHz
- **GPU:** AMD Radeon 890M, up to 2900 MHz
- **Display:** 7" LTPS, 1920x1080, 120 Hz + VRR + AMD FreeSync, 299 PPI
- **RAM:** 32 GB or 64 GB LPDDR5X
- **Storage:** 1/2/4 TB NVMe PCIe 4.0 2280
- **Battery:** 44.24 Wh (Cinebench loop: 1 hr 8 min; average: 3-6 hours)
- **I/O:** 1x USB 4.0, 1x USB-C 3.2 Gen2, 1x USB-A 3.2 Gen2, Micro SD
- **Connectivity:** WiFi 6E, Bluetooth 5.3
- **Weight:** 555 g
- **Dimensions:** 6.7 x 4.2 x 1 inches (17.2 x 10.9 x 2.7 cm) — marginally larger than 2023/2024
- **Price:** $1,315.95-$2,587.95

## Thermals

- Fan noise: 64 dB peak
- Temperature: 55°C max

## Benchmark Results

| Benchmark | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| PCMark | Third among HX 370 devices (behind WIN 4 and MAX 2) |
| 3DMark Time Spy | 3,879 |
| 3DMark Night Raid | 28,959 |
| 3DMark Fire Strike | 7,793 |

Cinebench 2024: typical single-core; lower multi-core (matching ONEXFLY F1 Pro HX 365).
Geekbench 6: near-identical single-core; second in multi-core.

## Gaming Benchmarks

| Game | Key Results |
|------|------------|
| Forza Horizon 5 | Matches other GPD HX 370 at 1080p; leads at 720p high TDP |
| Shadow of the Tomb Raider | Shared first at 1080p; leads at 720p until 10 W |
| Cyberpunk 2077 | Ties for first at 1080p; leads at 720p until 10 W |

**Pattern:** Dominant at 28 W TDP; loses some edge at 10 W.

## Real-World Gaming

- Palworld: 1080p Low 60+ FPS at 28 W
- No Man's Sky: 720p High 60 FPS
- Hades II: 1080p High — great for 120 Hz
- Lonely Mountain: 720p Low for 120 FPS, or higher for 60
- Overgrown: 1080p FSR Balanced 60+ FPS

## Emulation

Up to PS3 era flawless. DuckStation, PCSX2, RPCS3, Vita3K all excellent with enhanced resolutions.

## eGPU

No Oculink port, but USB 4 eGPU support. Cyberpunk: well above 90 FPS with GPD G1.

## Verdict

Great balance of portability (clamshell fits coat pocket) and performance. Strong at 28 W but dips at low TDP. 120 Hz display is a standout. Price: $1,315.95-$2,587.95.
