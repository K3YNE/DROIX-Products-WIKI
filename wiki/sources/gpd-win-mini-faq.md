## Frequently Asked Questions

### GPD WIN Mini

* How does the performance compare to other handheld PCs?

  The GPD WIN Mini offers strong performance for its size, particularly the 8840U model. It can handle most modern games at 720p-1080p with playable framerates. Performance is generally comparable to or slightly below larger handhelds with similar specs.
* Does the GPD WIN Mini have a touchscreen?

  Yes, the GPD WIN Mini features a 10-point capacitive touchscreen
* What’s the screen size and resolution of the GPD WIN Mini?

  The GPD WIN Mini has a 7-inch 1920×1080 (Full HD) IPS display with a 120Hz refresh rate.
* Does the GPD WIN Mini have external GPU support?

  Yes, the GPD WIN Mini supports external GPUs via its USB4 port. It’s compatible with eGPU enclosures like the GPD G1.
* Can I upgrade the storage or RAM on the GPD WIN Mini?

  The storage is upgradeable via the M.2 2230 SSD slot. The RAM is soldered and cannot be upgraded.
* What’s the battery life like on the GPD WIN Mini?

  The GPD WIN Mini has a 44.24Wh battery that provides approximately:

  + Up to 3 hours of heavy gaming use
  + 6-8 hours of moderate use
  + Up to 14 hours of light use

  Actual battery life will vary depending on usage and settings.
* What are the key specifications of the GPD WIN Mini?

  The GPD WIN Mini comes in two main variants:

  + GPD WIN Mini 2024 (8840U): AMD Ryzen 7 8840U processor, up to 64GB RAM, up to 2TB storage
  + GPD WIN Mini 2023 (7640U/7840U): AMD Ryzen 5 7640U or Ryzen 7 7840U processor, up to 32GB RAM, up to 2TB storage

  Both feature a 7-inch 1080p 120Hz display, built-in game controls, and run Windows 11
* Does the GPD WIN Mini support external displays.

  The GPD WIN Mini series has a USB-C port that you can use with a hub for HDMI output to a TV or [portable monitor](https://gpdstore.net/category/portable-monitors/). See our [accessories](https://gpdstore.net/category/accessories/) for compat ible hubs.
* There were reports of GPD WIN Mini 2023 devices being too hot to hold. Is this true?

  The original GPD WIN Mini 2023 review samples sent to reviewers were based on a prototype and did have an issue where the area around the D-Pad and gaming buttons was too hot to hold your fingers on. The final 2023 model which was sent to consumers has been fixed to resolve this issue. It will still get warm, all high performance handhelds will, but the temperatures are within acceptible limits and not cause discomfort. This is also applicible to the GPD WIN Mini 2024 which has no issues with temperature.
