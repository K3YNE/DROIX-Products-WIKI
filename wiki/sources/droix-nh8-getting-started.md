# Getting Started with the DROIX NH8 USB Hub with NVMe

The [[droix-nh8|DROIX NH8 USB Hub]] enhances your handheld gaming experience with NVMe storage expansion, 4K gaming at 60Hz, and a 1000Mbps Ethernet connection.

At [[droix|DROIX]] (GPD Store), we strongly support the user's Right to Repair. If you encounter any issues and feel confident in your ability to fix them, we encourage you to do so! Customer Support is always available for assistance.

## What's in the Box?

Handle the USB hub carefully to prevent any damage. Inside the box, you should find:

* 1x DROIX NH8 USB Hub

If anything is missing or unclear, contact DROIX Customer Service.

## Testing the DROIX NH8 USB Hub with NVMe

Run tests to ensure everything is functioning as expected. If any specific test fails, try using multiple cables to determine if the issue lies with the cable or the NH8 hub itself. During troubleshooting, make sure all cables are securely connected to the correct ports.

### NH8 USB Hub Tests

* **Physical Damage:** Inspect the hub's outer casing for any signs of damage.
* **Device Connection:** Connect your device to the NH8 Hub. To confirm the device is recognized, you can perform either the PD port test or the HDMI test.
* **USB-C PD Port:** Plug your charger into the PD port at the back of the hub to ensure your device is charging properly.
* **USB-C Port:** Connect your peripherals to the USB-C port located at the back of the hub and confirm that they are functioning correctly.
* **USB-A 3.0 Port:** Test different USB devices by plugging them into the USB-A ports and ensure each one is recognized and working as expected.
* **HDMI Port:** Power on your handheld device and display, then switch to the correct HDMI input. If an image appears on the display, the HDMI port is functioning properly.
* **Ethernet Port:** Connect an ethernet cable to the hub and your router or modem. Check if your handheld device has internet access via the ethernet connection to ensure the port is working.

If any issues persist, contact DROIX Customer Support for further assistance.

## What You Can Do with the NH8 Hub

Make sure the NH8 Hub is positioned in a secure spot. Take care to avoid any damage to both the USB hub and your connected handheld device.

### Connect Your Handheld Device

Begin by connecting your handheld gaming device using the USB Type-C cable attached to the NH8.

### Power & Charge Your Device

Ensure your handheld gaming PC or mini laptop is powered and charged for those long gaming sessions. Connect a charger to the USB-C PD Port located on the back of the hub. Note that while there are two USB-C ports, only one is specifically for charging, located next to the power LED. When properly connected, the power LED will display a solid light. If the LED flashes, the power cable is likely plugged into the wrong port.

**The charger is not included with the hub -- use the charger that comes with your handheld device or consider investing in a 100W adapter for faster charging.**

### Connect Your Display

To enhance your gaming experience on a larger screen, connect an HDMI cable between your NH8 hub and your display (TV, monitor, or other compatible display). Make sure to change the display input source to the correct HDMI input if needed. The NH8 supports up to 4K resolution at a 60Hz refresh rate. Please note, the HDMI cable is not included in the packaging.

### USB-C Ports

The DROIX NH8 USB Hub is equipped with 2x USB-C ports located on the back of the device. These ports allow you to connect peripheral devices like controllers, headsets, keyboards, mice, or other USB Type-C accessories. Not only does this enhance your gaming experience, but it also transforms your Windows handheld device into a more versatile tool for browsing the internet, watching videos, shopping online, and general PC activities.

### USB-A 3.0 Ports

The DROIX NH8 USB Hub includes 1x USB-A 3.0 port located at the back of the device. Like the USB-C ports, this port opens up even more possibilities for connecting peripherals or other USB Type-A devices.

### Ultra-Fast Internet Connection

For a fast, stable, and reliable internet connection, plug an ethernet cable directly into the hub, reducing latency and boosting performance in online gaming and media streaming. The ethernet port is located on the back of the NH8 Hub. Please note that the ethernet cable is not included.

### SD & MicroSD Card

SD cards offer a convenient way to expand your storage, making it easy for gamers to carry large game libraries and media collections in a compact form. With the capability to swiftly transfer files and manage content across devices, you can expand your storage and install games effortlessly. The NH8 hub features both SD and MicroSD card slots.

## NVMe Installation

The NH8 USB-C Hub offers additional storage expansion through its PCI-e 22*80 NVMe slot. NVMe support provides a substantial upgrade in both storage capacity and speed, with faster game load times, quick data transfers, and ample room for a vast game library -- all while preserving your device's internal memory.

To install an NVMe into the NH8 Hub:

1. Ensure the DROIX logo is facing upwards.
2. Open the NVMe enclosure.
3. Remove the rubber screws.
4. Carefully insert the NVMe. *Tip: Insert the NVMe at an upward angle.*
5. Secure the NVMe into the NH8 using the rubber screw. *Tip: Before pushing the NVMe down, place a rubber screw on it.*
6. Close the NVMe enclosure.

## Device Settings

### Windows Display Settings

The NH8 Hub supports up to 4K resolution and a smooth 60Hz refresh rate through its HDMI port. If both your device and display support higher resolutions, it is recommended to set them to the maximum resolution and refresh rate available for an enhanced gaming experience.

Keep in mind that increasing the resolution and refresh rate can lead to higher battery consumption, which is important when using your device in handheld mode. To optimize performance and battery life, consider adjusting these settings based on your current usage needs.

**To adjust your settings in Windows 11:**

1. Type "Display Settings" in the Windows search bar and open it.
2. Change the Display Resolution to the highest available option.
3. Select **Keep Changes**.
4. Go to **Advanced Display Settings**.
5. Change the Refresh Rate to the highest available option.
6. Select **Keep Changes**.
