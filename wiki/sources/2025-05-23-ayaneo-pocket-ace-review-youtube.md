# AYANEO Pocket ACE Review

Review published 2025-05-23 covering the [[ayaneo|AYANEO]] Pocket ACE, an Android gaming handheld powered by the **Snapdragon G3X Gen 2** chipset.


## Key Specifications

- **SoC**: **Snapdragon G3X Gen 2**
- **Display**: 4.5" IPS, 1620x1080, 60Hz
- **Weight**: 310g
- **Battery**: 6000mAh (2h55m in stress testing)
- **Controls**: Hall effect sticks and triggers
- **Connectivity**: USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-C
- **OS**: Android 13

## Performance and Emulation

The Pocket ACE emerged as the benchmark champion among devices sharing the same generation of hardware. Emulation performance handles up to PS2-era titles well. PlayStation Vita and Nintendo Switch emulation results are mixed, with some titles running acceptably and others struggling.

## Design and Build

The device strikes a strong balance between portability and performance at just 310g. The 4.5" screen keeps the form factor compact. The display is IPS at 60Hz rather than OLED, which is a tradeoff for the price point. Hall effect analog sticks and triggers provide precise, drift-free input.

## Comparisons

Compared favorably against the **AYANEO Pocket S** and **AYANEO Pocket DMG** within AYANEO's own lineup. The Pocket ACE positions itself as a performance-focused option in a compact form factor.

Available at [[droix|DROIX]] at the time of review.
