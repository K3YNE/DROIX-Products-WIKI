# ONEXPLAYER ONEXFLY Apex Review

Review published 2026-03-04 covering the [[onexplayer|ONEXPLAYER]] ONEXFLY Apex, a high-performance Windows gaming handheld described as a "beast of a machine." Powered by the [[amd-ryzen-ai-max|AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395]].


## Key Specifications

- **CPU/GPU**: [[amd-ryzen-ai-max|AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395]] (same chip as [[gpd-win-5|GPD WIN 5]])
- **Display**: 8" 1200p, 120Hz, VRR
- **Weight**: 706g (standard) + 387g external battery = 1093g total
- **Storage**: Mini SSD support with 2x PCIe lanes
- **Cooling**: Available in standard or liquid cooled variants

## Performance

Performance matches or slightly beats the [[gpd-win-5|GPD WIN 5]]. Mini SSD speeds reach 3551MB/s compared to 1785MB/s on the WIN 5 thanks to double PCIe lanes. Capable of running Cyberpunk 2077 with ray tracing, Doom at nightmare settings, and Warhammer at high settings.

## Design and Build

The device is heavy at 706g before accounting for the external battery. Combined weight of 1093g causes hand fatigue during longer gaming sessions. The 8" display provides a large viewing area for Windows gaming.

## Comparisons

Directly compared with the [[gpd-win-5|GPD WIN 5]] which shares the same [[amd-ryzen-ai-max|AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395]] processor. The ONEXFLY Apex edges ahead in SSD speeds and slightly in overall performance benchmarks.

## Availability

Currently USA-only at [[droix|DROIX]] at the time of review.
