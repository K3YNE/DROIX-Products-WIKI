## Summary

The AYANEO AM02 is a Windows mini PC from [[ayaneo]], reviewed by DROIX in February 2024. It is notable for incorporating a built-in 4-inch display, distinguishing it from typical mini PCs and reflecting AYANEO's approach of bringing handheld-style convenience to a desktop-adjacent form factor. The AM02 handles light-to-medium 4K gaming (e.g., 60 FPS on Go Mecha Ball at 4K via Xbox Game Pass) and 1080p gaming at medium-high settings (e.g., Forza Horizon 5 hovering around 70 FPS). It also delivers strong emulation performance across PS2, PS3, Switch, and Xbox 360. DROIX stocked the AM02 as part of its AYANEO mini PC range.

## Design & Build

The AM02 is a compact mini PC chassis with a built-in 4-inch display — a signature AYANEO design choice for their AM-series mini PCs. The device connects to an external display for primary use while the built-in screen provides secondary information or system monitoring capability.

## Gaming & Emulation Performance

From the DROIX review:

- **Forza Horizon 5:** 1080p, mix of Medium/High settings — approximately 70 FPS, smooth with no issues
- **Go Mecha Ball:** 4K, solid 60 FPS (lightweight title from Xbox Game Pass)
- **PS2 (PCSX2/AetherSX2):** Strong performance; internal rendering resolution can be increased on most games, limited on demanding titles like Outrun
- **PS3 (RPCS3):** Very good overall; occasional shader caching and missing textures on first run, smooth thereafter
- **Switch (Yuzu/Ryujinx):** Decent; not all games run perfectly but a good number are playable
- **Xbox 360 (Xenia):** Around 30 FPS on Project Gotham Racing 2; some playable titles
