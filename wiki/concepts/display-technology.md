# Display Technology

Display choice is a key differentiator across handheld gaming devices. Panel type, resolution, refresh rate, and aspect ratio all influence the gaming experience.

## Panel Types

### AMOLED / OLED
Best colors, deepest blacks, highest contrast. Found on premium devices:
- [[ayn-thor|AYN Thor]] — both screens AMOLED
- [[ayn-odin-3|AYN Odin 3]] — AMOLED
- [[ayaneo-flip-1s-ds|AYANEO Flip 1S DS]] — top screen OLED
- [[ayaneo-pocket-ds|AYANEO Pocket DS]] — top screen OLED

### IPS
Vibrant colors, wide viewing angles, high quality without the OLED premium:
- [[ayaneo-pocket-s2|AYANEO Pocket S2]] — IPS panel
- [[ayaneo-pocket-ace|AYANEO Pocket ACE]] — IPS panel

### LCD
Budget-friendly, functional. Found on entry-level devices:
- [[konkr-pocket-fit|KONKR Pocket FIT]] — LCD with 144Hz refresh rate
- [[anbernic-rg-slide|Anbernic RG Slide]] — LCD

### LTPS
A specialized panel type suited for pixel-perfect retro upscaling:
- [[ayaneo-pocket-vert|AYANEO Pocket VERT]] — LTPS display, chosen specifically for clean integer scaling of retro content

## Resolution Range

Resolutions across reviewed devices span from compact to high-density:

- **1024x768** — [[ayaneo-pocket-ds|AYANEO Pocket DS]] bottom screen
- Up to **2560x1600** — [[onexplayer-g1|ONEXPLAYER G1]]

Higher resolution is not always better on small screens — it demands more GPU power and can reduce battery life without visible benefit at handheld viewing distances.

## Refresh Rate

- **60Hz** — standard, found on most devices (e.g., [[ayaneo-pocket-s2|AYANEO Pocket S2]])
- **120Hz** — mid-tier upgrade for smoother gameplay
- **144Hz** — [[konkr-pocket-fit|KONKR Pocket FIT]]
- **165Hz** — highest seen in reviewed devices

### Higher Hz vs Better Panel

A key tradeoff exists between refresh rate and panel quality. The [[konkr-pocket-fit|KONKR Pocket FIT]] offers 144Hz on an LCD panel, while the [[ayaneo-pocket-s2|AYANEO Pocket S2]] runs at 60Hz on a higher-quality IPS panel. For many users, the better colors and contrast of a superior panel outweigh the smoothness advantage of a higher refresh rate.

## Aspect Ratios

- **16:9** — standard widescreen, most common across the lineup
- **4:3** — [[anbernic-rg-slide|Anbernic RG Slide]], ideal for retro content (NES, SNES, GBA, PS1) which was designed for 4:3 displays
- **3:2 vertical** — [[ayaneo-pocket-vert|AYANEO Pocket VERT]], providing 10x the resolution of the original Game Boy screen. Excellent for vertical-oriented retro games.
