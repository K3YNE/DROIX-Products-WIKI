## Device Overview

Next in our [GPD WIN Mini 2024](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-4-2024/) review, we examine the handheld gaming PC closely. The GPD WIN Mini 2024 measures around 6.6 x 4.2 x 1.02 inches (16.8 x 10.9 x 2.6 cm) and weighs 520g. The lid flips up to reveal a 7” [LTPS](https://gpdstore.net/product-attribute/display-type/ltps/) touchscreen display with a 1920×1080 resolution and a variable refresh rate of 60Hz and 120Hz. This native landscape panel ensures compatibility with older games.

The lower half features standard gaming controls, including hall effect analogues and a backlit QWERTY keyboard. While the keyboard is compact and not comparable to a laptop-sized keyboard, it is usable with thumb or one-finger typing. The buttons require some pressure to press, so it isn’t ideal for long typing sessions, but it works well for brief messages and logging into games or websites.

Along the top, the device includes shoulder and trigger buttons, a USB 4 port, and a USB Type-C port. The USB 4 port can connect to an eGPU such as the [GPD G1](https://gpdstore.net/accessories/gpd-g1/). There is also a toggle switch for changing between gamepad and mouse input, a micro SD card slot, and a USB A port for peripherals. The OcuLink port from last year’s model is missing in the 2024 version.

## System Benchmarks

As part of our [GPD WIN Mini 2024](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-4-2024/) review, we ran various system benchmarks to compare with the GPD WIN 4 2024 handheld gaming PC, which also features the 8840U processor, as well as some previous generation 7840U series portable gaming PCs.

### **PCMark**

PCMark tests the day-to-day and productivity tasks. It’s in the higher end of the results, although slightly lower than the GPD WIN 4 2024.

* Score: 6,841.

PCMark Benchmark Results

### **3DMark**

[3DMark](https://www.3dmark.com/) is an industry standard for benchmarking the CPU single and multi-core performance. For Time Spy we get a performance of 2,953 with the GPD WIN Mini, which is ever so slightly lower than expected. For Night Raid we see scores on the lower end of the scale. And for Fire Strike we again see slightly lower than expected scores.

* Time Spy: 2,953 (7,705 with the GPD G1).
* Night Raid: 25,859 (31,602 with the GPD G1). Slightly lower scores than expected.
* Fire Strike: 6,893 (18,297 with the GPD G1). Also, slightly lower than expected.

3DMark Benchmark Results

### **Cinebench**

Single-core performance matches the [Win 4 2024](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-4-2024/), but there is a drop in multi-core performance. Overall, this is a good score.

* Single-Core: 1,717
* Multi-Core: 11,554.

Cinebench Benchmark Results

### Thermals and Fan Noise

Powering the device is a 44.24Wh battery, which in our tests running Cinebench on a loop lasted around 1 hour 38 minutes, about 18 minutes longer than the Win 4. Expect around 6-8 hours of average usage. In our fan noise and temperature tests, while running Cinebench, the highest fan noise recorded was 68dB, and the highest temperature reached was 59 °C.

## Gaming Benchmarks

We performed gaming benchmarks at 1080P and 720P resolutions and at various TDP settings (5, 10, 15, 20, and 28W) to get a range of results for comparison. Although missing an OcuLink port, we connected an eGPU via the USB 4 port and ran brief tests with the [GPD G1 2024](https://gpdstore.net/accessories/gpd-g1/).

### **Forza Horizon 5**

Running on Very Low graphics settings. Good results at 720P, but performance drops at higher TDP compared to the [GPD Win 4 2024](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-4-2024/). At 1080P, average frames per second are lower.

Forza Horizon 5 Benchmark Results

### **Shadow of the Tomb Raider**

Running on the Lowest graphics settings. Lower frames per second at 1080P compared to the Win 4 2024, improving as TDP decreases.

Shadow of the Tomb Raider Benchmark Results

### **Call of Duty Modern Warfare III**

Running on Minimal graphics settings with no FSR enabled. Very good results overall.

Call of Duty Modern Warfare III Benchmark Results

### **Street Fighter VI**

Running on the highest graphics settings. Lower performance at 1080P 28W TDP compared to the [GPD WIN 4 2024](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-4-2024/) and even the [original GPD WIN Mini](https://gpdstore.net/gpd-handheld-gaming-pcs/gpd-win-mini/) compact gaming PC. The difference is only a frame or two but noticeable.
