## Anbernic RG 40XXV Overview

Let’s get the Anbernic RG 40XXV review underway with an overview. The [Anbernic RG 40XXV](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg-40xxv/) classic gaming handheld is available in three colours; White, Transparent Black and Indigo Blue.

Choice of White Black Transparent or Indigo Blue

It measures around 5.47 x 3.6 x 0.86 inches (13.9 x 9..2 x 2.2 cm) and weighs 216g (0.47 lbs).

There’s a great looking small bezel 4” [IPS](https://droix.net/product-attribute/display-type/ips/) screen with a 640×480 resolution. The picture quality is very impressive as you will see when we get some games up and running.

Anbernic RG 40XXV Front View

On the front of the [Anbernic RG 40XXV](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg-40xxv/) we have a D-Pad and gaming buttons along with a menu button. There’s a single analogue stick surrounded by RGB lighting. You can customise this in the settings to change the lighting mode, brightness and speed, or switch it off if it’s not your thing.

Bottom View



Left View



Right View

Top View

On the left side are volume buttons and the first of two micro SD card slots.  On the right are the power button, reset button and the second micro SD card slot. On the top is a mini HDMI port for outputting to a TV or monitor.  The bottom of the portable retro gaming console has a USB-C port for charging and a 3.5mm audio jack.

Back View

And last but not least the back has four buttons to act as shoulder and trigger buttons. They are comfortable to rest your fingers on acting as a grips of sorts while they are not in use.

## OS Overview

Next in our Anbernic RG 40XXV review we cover the operating system and frontend features. We see the now familiar Linux based frontend that [Anbernic](https://droix.net/product-attribute/brands/anbernic/) have been using in their portable gaming handhelds this year. There have been improvements over time which is great to see. You have two menus for native Linux based emulators and the other for RetroArch.

Emulators galore

Supported on the [Anbernic RG 40XXV](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg-40xxv/) are systems ranging from Atari 2600 up to PS1 and PSP. Performance and compatibility can vary so one emulator may be better than the other. Generally the RetroArch emulator cores work great and have easier to use features such as save states, fast forward, screenshots etc.

## Emulation Performance

All the 8 and 16 bit systems should run fine without any issues at all, everything up to the PS1 era works great on this mobile gaming handheld.

PS1 Crash Bandicoot 3

You will notice with N64 and Dreamcast that performance is hit and miss depending on the game. You will need to use frame skipping to get more demanding games playable.

Dreamcast Crazy Taxi

PSP is also supported, but again performance is mixed. The low demanding games will be playable, medium maybe with frame skipping, but you can forget about higher demanding games like God of War, even with frame skipping.

PSP GTA Vice City

Worth mentioning in our Anbernic RG 40XXV review are custom firmwares. There are the excellent [MinUI](https://github.com/shauninman/MinUI) and [Knulli](https://knulli.org/) custom firmwares available if you want to squeeze some extra performance out of the [Anbernic RG 40XXV](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg-40xxv/). As well as improving performance you also get nicer looking frontends and a wealth of options and features. Knulli should be back available soon once some bugs are fixed.

## Final Thoughts

Street Fighter II



Rick Dangerous Port



Sonic Advance

Time to sum up our thoughts in this Anbernic RG 40XXV review. The [Anbernic RG 40XXV](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg-40xxv/) is overall a decent handheld. The 4” display really does look great and for me is just the right size for a vintage gaming handheld. Personally I think it could do without the analogue stick, it’s really only required for the later supported systems which have mixed success in terms of performance.

While the stock firmware has improved over the past months, we do need to look again at custom firmwares to get the best out of these handhelds. But for the price versus a good quality handheld with larger 4” display, it is great value for money. If you are in the market for a vertical handheld, then look no further!
