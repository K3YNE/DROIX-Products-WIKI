## GMKTec NucBox M4’s Technical Specifications

Under the hood, the [NucBox M4](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m4/) is powered by the 11th Gen Intel [Core i9-11900H](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/core-i9-11900hh/) processor, featuring 8 cores and 16 threads, with a max turbo frequency of 4.9GHz and 24MB Intel Smart Cache. It is paired with Intel [Iris Xe Graphics](https://droix.net/product-attribute/graphics-gpu-model/iris-xe-graphics/), supporting up to 1.45GHz with 80 execution units. The system comes with either 16GB or 32GB of [DDR4](https://droix.net/product-attribute/memory-ram-technology/ddr4-so-dimm/) RAM, expandable up to 128GB. Storage options include a 512GB or 1TB SSD, with support for up to 4TB via M.2 2280 PCIe 4.0. The mini PC runs on Windows 11 Pro and supports Linux, offering versatile OS compatibility.

## Build and Design

The [NucBox M4](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m4/) boasts a sleek and sturdy metal case, ensuring durability and efficient heat dissipation. Measuring 7.3 x 7.1 x 3.7 inches and weighing 959 grams, it is designed for convenience and portability. The front panel includes a combo headphone/microphone jack, a Type-C port with display support, two USB 3.2 Type-A ports, and a power button. The rear features additional connectivity options with a DisplayPort, HDMI port, four USB 3.2 ports, two 2.5G LAN ports, and a power socket. The device also supports a Kensington lock for added security. This design makes it an excellent mini PC for home office and mini PC for business environments.

## Benchmark Results and Performance

#### System Benchmarks

The NucBox M4 performs admirably in benchmark tests, reflecting its high-performance components. In Geekbench, it scores 1670 in single-core and 8898 in multi-core tests, highlighting its processing power. The PCMark 10 test, designed to measure overall system performance, gives the [NucBox M4](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m4/) a score of 6200, indicating strong capabilities in productivity and digital content creation tasks.

#### Gaming and Emulation Benchmarks

Despite its powerful CPU, the NucBox M4’s integrated Intel Iris Xe Graphics has limitations in gaming. In 3DMark, it scores around 7500, performing adequately in less demanding games. For instance, it achieves an average of 26 FPS in GTA V at 1080p on very high settings. More graphically intensive games like A Plague Tale and Undisputed struggle, with frame rates dropping to 15-19 FPS at lower settings. Emulation performance is satisfactory, handling older console games smoothly but struggling with newer systems.
