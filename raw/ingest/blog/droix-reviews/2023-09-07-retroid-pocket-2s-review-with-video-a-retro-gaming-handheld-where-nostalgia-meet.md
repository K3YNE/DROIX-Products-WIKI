## Retroid Pocket 2S Overview

Continuing our Retroid Pocket 2S review with an overview of the device. The [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/) measures around 6.1 x 3.14 x 1.1 inches at its largest  (15.5 x 8 x 2.8 cm) and it weighs around 196g. It is available in six colours; Clear Green and 16bit both shown here, as well as Retro, Black, Indigo and Clear Back.

Retroid Pocket 2S Front View

The display is a 3.5” touchscreen with a 4:3 aspect ratio and 640×480 resolution which is great for retrogaming and upscaling.

On the front we have a classic D-Pad with hall effect analogue sticks and the usual gaming buttons. Along the bottom of the portable gaming handheld there are three additional buttons for Home, Return and a custom button.

Retroid Pocket 2S Top View

Along the top we have left and right shoulder and trigger buttons, a Micro HDMI port for outputting to the TV or monitor, Type-C port for charging, a volume rocker and the power button.

Retroid Pocket 2S Bottom View

On the bottom there is a micro SD card slot with rubber cap, and a 3.5mm headphone port.

## Retroid Pocket 2S Android Overview

The Pocket 2S runs on Android 11 which most of these handhelds usually run on. The [Anbernic RG405V](https://droix.net/product/anbernic-rg405v/) has set the trend running Android 12 though. We will spend a few moments with an overview of the Android OS for this Retroid Pocket 2S review.

Retroid Pocket 2S Startup Wizard

The now familiar startup wizard greets you when first booting up and will go through a few setup steps such as connecting to WiFi and if you want Google Play Store installed. You can also choose which apps and emulators you want to install to the [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/) for you. It’s a decent wizard and gives you the choice of a clean or partial setup install.

Android Launcher

You have a choice of using the standard Android menu or the Retroid Launcher which does require some setting up to use. You can scan your storage for any games you have added and it will sort them into appropriate consoles and download the images etc.

Retroid Pocket 2S Screen mapper

You also have the overlay software which is very good. From here you can quickly and easily change various settings, take screenshots, record video and livestream. You also have the very good screen mapping software where you can map the game controls to on screen controls.

Top marks for [Retroid](https://droix.net/product-attribute/brands/retroid/) and their Android handheld setup!

## System Benchmarks

As part of our Retroid Pocket 2S review we are running some system benchmarks so we can compare the performance with other devices.

### 3DMark

3DMark Benchmark Results

We start the benchmarks with 3DMark, it tests the CPU and GPU working together for its performance. The [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/) vintage gaming handheld is sitting roughly between the Pocket Flip and the Pocket 3 models with a score of 1482.

### Geekbench

Geekbench Benchmark Results

Next we have Geekbench which tests single and multi-core CPU performance. For single core performance we see scores not far from the Tiger T618 processor. However in multi-core performance we can see a higher performance difference between the T618 and T610 in the [Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/).

### Antutu

Antutu Benchmark Results

And last but not least Antutu which performs your more day-to-day usage tasks from web browsing, UI performance and media playing.

We see great scores for the [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/), even beating the Pocket Flip portable gaming handheld. I am not sure what made the difference here, maybe the faster RAM speed but it definitely helped.

## Emulator Performance

And of course we could not have a Retroid Pocket 2S review without the emulator performance. We know that the overall performance is a little lower than the Tiger T618 models but this won’t affect emulation performance so much up past the usual PlayStation 1 and Dreamcast era.

DuckStation PlayStation 1 emulator

We tried a bunch of PS1 and Dreamcast games and had no issues, everything was full frames and blue skies!

Dolphin emulator

Moving on to the Dolphin emulator you will find some performance issues on some of the more demanding games. You can change the rendering resolution a notch or two down to improve the performance.

For PS2 you won’t have as much success as the T618 Android handhelds. We did see some performance issues in games that run fine on Retroid Pocket 3+ and [Retroid Pocket Flip](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-flip/). Again you can lower the rendering resolution to improve performance but don’t expect amazing results.

Citra emulator

We actually saw fairly good performance on the Citra emulator. Naturally you will have to  play most games through to generate the shaders, but after that we saw Sonic Generations and Ridge Racer running fairly well. You will see more often freezing than the T618 devices but overall it’s not bad performance.

## Final Thoughts

We finish our Retroid Pocket 2S review with our thoughts on the device. Overall the [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/) retro gaming handheld is a good addition to the Retroid range. It’s around 25% lower in cost, with around 22% lower performance than the Pocket Flip and [Retroid Pocket 3+](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-3-plus/) models. It is decent value for money in terms of price versus performance. Essentially you are missing out on some of the higher demanding emulators which even on the T618 processors was not perfect.

AetherSX2 emulator

We like the form factor of the Pocket 2S, it is comfortable to hold and definitely portable, maybe not quite as good as the 3+ model but it’s still nice. Having a newer version of Android, as well as the Retroid software is good to have, so no complaints there.

The [Retroid Pocket 2S](https://droix.net/product/retroid-pocket-2s/) classic gaming handheld is definitely worth considering if you are on a budget and not that fussed about emulators after the PlayStation 1 and Dreamcast era.
