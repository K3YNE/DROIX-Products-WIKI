We are taking a brief look at the official [AYANEO Next docking station](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-next-docking-station/).

## AYANEO Next & Next Pro docking station video

Opening the box we have a quick guide which show the devices functions and specifications

Docking station guide

Underneath is the docking station itself. There are two colours to choose from; Dark Star and Light Moon.

Dark star light moon colours

The docking station lid flips up to reveal the USB Type-C which connects to your AYANEO Next or Pro. There is a charging status indicator LED in front which shows if the device is charging etc.

AYANEO Next and Next Pro docking station front

On the back there is a USB Type-C port and two USB 3.1 ports for connecting peripherals to. The second USB Type-C port is used to connect the charger to. There is a HDMI out port and last but not least there is a gigabit ethernet port.

AYANEO Next and Next Pro docking station back

Once you have your cables plugged in, the status LED will show green. You simply plug in the [AYANEO Next Pro](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-next-pro/), wait a few moments for connection and any attached peripherals will now be usable. The LED will change colour to indicate if it is charging.

Docking station docked

If you have connected a HDMI cable, the display will also show the desktop. By default it is set to screen mirroring. You can change it in the Windows display settings as a second monitor, or switch off the AYANEO screen and use the monitor as the main display.

You can switch between handheld and external controls in a matter of seconds, and just as easily undock and resume playing just fine.

Playing on a TV controller while docked

As a note, this is only for the [AYANEO Next](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-next/) and [Next Pro](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-next-pro/) handhelds. Older models, or the new [AYANEO Air](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-air/) model will not fit the dock.
