## Overview of the GMKTec NucBox7

The [GMKTec NucBox 7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) mini PC measures around 3.4 x 3.4 x 1.5 inches (8.7 x 8.7 x 3.9 cm) and weighs 214 grams. On the front is the power button. There is a thin LED light strip surrounding the device and you can change between seven colours in the BIOS.

## System Benchmarks

### PassMark

As part of our NucBox7 review we perform some system benchmarks to find out its performance and compare it to other mini PC’s.

PassMark Benchmark Results

PassMark pushes the CPU, GPU, RAM and storage to their maximum in a series of tests. The GMKTec NucBox7 scores 1,828. We will be comparing this scores with other [mini PC’s](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-pcs/) after the benchmarks.

### PCMark

PCMark Benchmark Results

PCMark is a series of more natural tests covering day to day tasks from web browsing to image processing. The [NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) scores a very impressive 2,648, it’s the highest score in our budget range of mini PCs.

### 3DMark

3DMark Benchmark Results

While the NucBox7 is not a gaming mini PC, 3DMark tests the CPU and GPU to see how well they work together in video processing tasks. It’s also useful for decoding videos for example. The [GMKTec NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) scores 409 on the Time Spy test.

### Cinebench

Cinebench Benchmark Results

Cinebench tests the CPU to see its performance with either single or multi-core tests. The NucBox7 scores 1,824 on the multi-core score.

## Gaming Performance

We know that the [NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) is great for office type tasks but it is far from being a gaming mini PC. Some less demanding games do run just fine, for example we have Turtles Shredder’s Revenge running at a solid 60 FPS with no issues.

Low demand games should run OK on the NucBox7

Where you will have more luck with AAA titles is game streaming. For example with Xbox Cloud Gaming we can stream AAA games over the internet. We spent some time testing this and had no issues running at the highest quality settings with no noticeable delays or video decoding artefacts. Simply plug in a gamepad such as the [EasySMX](https://droix.net/blogs/brand/easysmx/) range available on our store and you are gaming within seconds.

For game streaming the NucBox7 is excellent

## Emulation Performance

Despite it not being great for native gaming we do get some pretty good performance out of it for emulation. The [NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) can run the majority of systems up to the PlayStation 1 and Dreamcast era with no issues. Let’s take a look at the performance on some newer systems.

### Dolphin

We got great performance out of the Dolphin emulator for both consoles it emulates. We got full speed on games such as Burnout 2 so overall it runs pretty good.

Dolphin emulation is great

For the newer console it emulates we see a drop in performance on Sonic Colors which originally runs at 30FPS. We do get near that target frame rate but not constantly.

### PPSSPP

PSP emulation with PPSSPP is perfect

Our go to game God of War Chains of Olympus on the PPSSPP emulator is running at a stable 60FPS with some very minor dips now and again. If this game works fine then just about every other game should have no issues running.

### PCSX2

It is not quite fast enough for high demand PS2 games

The PlayStation 2 era is where we start to see performance issues. For less demanding games you should be able to get playable levels of performance. But for games such as OutRun Coast 2 Coast we are getting around 30 FPS and for Gran Turismo it was around 15 FPS.

### Citra

You will see some shader cache lag in demanding games

The [NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) has mixed success with the Citra emulator. We do see less demanding games running well. However for higher demanding games you will see some slowdown whilst the shader cache is generated on first play. The next time you play through it will be closer to full speed. Overall there is OK performance.

## Final Thoughts

We sum up our NucBox7 review with our thoughts on the NUC mini PC. The [NucBox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) has just enough going for it to warrant updating from the previous model. The extra RAM is a definite bonus that improves the overall performance of Windows and having multiple apps open.

It is perfect for your day to day work related tasks in the home and office. It takes next to no space on your desktop or mounted behind your display. It is also very portable, fitting in a jacket pocket or bag,  perfect if you are travelling between offices for presentations for example.

Halo Infinite on Xbox Cloud Gaming

While you would never be able to play AAA games natively, it is perfect as a game streaming device. And for emulation it is also very good, you can play up to PS1 and Dreamcast era with no issues, and have fairly good performance with some newer gaming systems.

Overall the [GMKTec Nucbox7](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox7/) is excellent value for money with the best performance in the budget mini PC range. It should be your number one choice if you are looking for a great performing, portable mini PC that won’t break the bank. And don’t forget the low power usage which is excellent for saving on energy bills!
