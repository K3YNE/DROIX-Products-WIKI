## AYANEO 2 Overview

The [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) handheld PC measures around 10.4 x 4.1 x 1.42 inches (26.45 x 10.55 x 3.61 cm) and weighs 680g. It will be available in four colours, Starry Black, Sky White, Retro Power shown here and B.Duck.

The display is a 7” touchscreen LCD with a native resolution of 1920×1200. The front of the handheld is covered with a single sheet of glass which supposedly has an anti-fingerprint coating.

AYANEO 2 Front View

All of your usual gaming controls are present, including LED lit hall sensor analogue sticks, a comfortable to use D-Pad and gaming buttons. There are two additional buttons which are shortcuts to the AYA SPACE overlay and desktop toggle.

Along the top are the shoulder buttons and hall sensor triggers. There are two small shortcut buttons which can be configured in the AYA SPACE software. There is a power button with a built-in fingerprint sensor and a volume rocker. And last but not least there are two USB Type-C ports.

AYANEO 2 Top View

On the bottom is a USB Type-C port, a micro SD card slot and a 3.5mm headphone jack.

AYANEO 2 Bottom View

## AYA SPACE

We continue our AYANEO 2 review with a quick mention for [AYA SPACE](https://droix.net/blogs/aya-space-official-frontend-for-the-aya-neo-and-aya-neo-pro/). The included AYA SPACE software is great to use and transforms the handheld into a more console like experience. Your installed games are shown on the Home and Games screen and you have shortcuts to commonly used settings. You can also customise the RGB lighting that is around the two analogue sticks.

Aya Space

While playing a game you tap the Aya key and it will bring up an overlay where you can access shortcuts such as changing the fan speed, resolution, TDP and more.

It is a very useful app and we are happy to see that it is constantly being worked on adding new features.

## System Benchmarks

We are running the benchmarks at 28W TDP to keep in line with our handheld gaming PC benchmark format. We will be comparing the results with the other handhelds at the end of the benchmarks.

### PCMark

PCMark runs a series of tests covering day to day tasks from web browsing to image processing. It gives us a good overview of the general performance.

PCMark Benchmark Result

The AYANEO 2 scores 6,384 which is in the same ballpark as the other handhelds.

### Cinebench

Cinebench tests the CPU to see its performance with either single or multi-core tests. For the multi-core test, the [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) portable gaming PC scores 10,989

Cinebench Benchmark Result

### 3DMark

The 3DMark benchmark tests the CPU and GPU to see how well they work together in video processing tasks.

3DMark Benchmark Result

For the Time Spy benchmark the [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) handheld PC scores 2,710 which is in line with other handhelds.

For Fire Strike we got a score of 6,689. And for Night Raid we got a score of 25,081

### CrystalDiskMark

CrystalDiskMark runs a series of tests on the storage to see its performance across different reading/writing patterns. We get a highest read speed of 2,573 MB/s and write speed of 1,775 MB/s.

## Games Performance

Continuing our AYANEO 2 review we will try some games at playable graphics levels for best visual quality and for best battery life. We are essentially using the same settings for games as the ONEXPLAYER Mini Pro due to the similar performance levels.

### Forza Horizon 5

Forza Horizon 5 Gameplay

With Forza Horizon 5 you can run at 1200P with a mix of Medium and High graphics settings. For best battery life you can go with 800P on Very Low graphics at 11W TDP.

### Overwatch 2

Overwatch 2 Gameplay

At 1200P 28W we can run Overwatch 2 on the High graphics settings with no upscaling. There may be some occasional drops below 60FPS but nothing that will ruin the gameplay. At 800P at 11W you can run the game just fine with the Low graphics settings.

### Doom Eternal

Doom Eternal Gameplay

For Doom Eternal we tried it at 12900P and a mix of Low and Medium settings for 60FPS. If you drop the resolution to 800P you can get up to High graphics settings. For best battery life you can run at 800P on Low graphics settings at 15W TDP

### Call of Duty Modern Warfare 2

CoD MW2 Gameplay

For the campaign we recommend 800P 28W on the Balanced graphics settings which will keep you above 60 for the most part. For the multiplayer mode you probably want as high FPS as possible so we went with 800P 28W on minimal graphics with no upscaling.

## Emulator Performance

Again the emulation performance is much the same as the other handhelds. You can run everything up to the PlayStation 2 era on the [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) handheld PC with no issues at all. You can even run them at lower TDP to save battery life. We will take a brief look at some of the newer systems to see how well they run.

### Xbox – Xemu emulator

OutRun 2 on Xbox emulator Xemu

Providing the games are compatible you should see very good performance on the Xbox emulator Xemu. We tried a few different games and had no issues with games running at their native full speed. But it does depend on the game being compatible.

### Citra emulator

Sonic Generations on Citra emulator

Performance is mixed on Citra. Games such as Sonic Generations will see shader caching lag upon first playthrough of the game, after that it is fine. Other games such as Ridge Racer 3D pretty much works fine on the first playthrough with some minor shader cache lags occasionally. Other less demanding games will run great.

### Xbox 360 Xenia emulator

Vanquish on Xbox 360 emulator

Compatible games work quite well on the Xbox 360 emulator Xenia. Vanquish, Sonic Racing Transformed and PGR2 run at full speed. On Halo 3 you may see some drops while the next part of the map is loading in. Overall the performance is very impressive!

### PS3 RPCS3 emulator

Wipeout HD on PS3

Again providing the game is compatible you get some great performance with near solid 60 FPS on many games. Tekken 6, Wipeout HD, OutRun Arcade and Skate 3 all work with next to no issues. If you are a PS3 fan then the 6800U based handhelds are perfect for you.

### Yuzu and Ryujinx emulators

Cruisn Blast on YuZu emulator

For YuZu and Ryujinx it again depends if the emulators support the game. If it does then you will generally get decent performance with games running at full or near full speed for third and first party games.

## Final Thoughts

Overall the [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) is very impressive. In terms of performance it’s the same as the other 6800U based [handheld PC’s](https://droix.net/blogs/product-category/handhelds/handheld-gaming-pcs/) which I am happy to see. You can run the latest AAA games and actually be able to play them. Though with some it may be on lower resolutions and graphics settings, but you can still play them. And for emulation, the 6800U processor is great with games running full speed on the 360 and PS3 amongst others. I have nothing negative to say about the performance at all.

Metal Hellsinger

The design of the AYANEO 2 is good, it feels comfortable in the hand and it’s not too heavy to play for long periods of time, though you will likely be resting on your lap or table while playing. The single sheet of glass covering the front is a nice touch, something we haven’t seen before. Talking about touches, it’s definitely not fingerprint proof as they claim.

Soccer Story

As mentioned earlier in the video it does come down to the design and features etc of the handheld as to which one you should buy. The [AYANEO 2](https://droix.net/product/ayaneo-2/) does have the excellent AYA SPACE which others either do not have or have relatively basic features in comparison. So this could be a deciding factor in your choice for example.
