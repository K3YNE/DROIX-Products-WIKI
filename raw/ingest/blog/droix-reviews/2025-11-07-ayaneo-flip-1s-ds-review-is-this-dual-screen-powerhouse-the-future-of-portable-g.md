## A Closer Look: Unboxing the AYANEO 1S DS

We begin our AYANEO 1S DS review with a close inspection of this portable gaming PC. The AYANEO 1S DS itself measures approximately 7.0 x 4.0 x 1.15 inches (18.0 x 10.2 x 2.93 cm). Furthermore, it weighs around 655g (1.44 lbs).

AYANEO Flip 1S DS Dimensions

Opening the AYANEO 1S DS reveals its primary 7-inch 1080P OLED display located at the top. This impressive screen supports multiple refresh rates, ranging from [60Hz](https://droix.net/product-attribute/refresh-rate/60hz/ "60Hz") up to a smooth [144Hz](https://droix.net/product-attribute/refresh-rate/144hz/ "144Hz"). Additionally, the hinge is quite flexible, allowing for any angle to be used, all the way up to 180 degrees.

AYANEO Flip 1S DS Top Display



AYANEO Flip 1S DS Bottom Display

On the lower half, we find all the usual gaming controls. These include dual analogue TMR sticks, a D-Pad, and standard action buttons.

Along the very bottom, a variety of controls are conveniently placed at your fingertips. This area features a bottom display mode control, an Xbox game button, volume buttons, and a power button with an integrated fingerprint sensor. Moreover, an optical finger mouse, AYA overlay button, and dedicated desktop buttons are also included.

In the middle sits the second display. This is an IPS panel measuring 4.5 inches and featuring a 1620×1080 resolution.

AYANEO Flip 1S DS Bottom Side View



AYANEO Flip 1S DS Top Side View



AYANEO Flip 1S DS Textured Grips

Looking along the top of the AYANEO 1S DS, you will find the left and right shoulder and trigger controls, as well as macro buttons. There is also a covered micro SD card reader. Alongside it, two USB 4 ports are provided for high-speed peripherals.

Finally, the back of the mobile gaming PC features two replaceable textured grips. One grip is bumpy, while the other offers more of a waved texture. You can easily change between these two options depending on your personal preference.

Powering this portable gaming computer is a 45Wh battery. The battery supports up to 65W PD charging and, importantly, also supports bypass charging.

In our testing, we achieved around 1 hour and 5 minutes of battery life. This test was conducted running Cinebench at a 28W TDP with full brightness. This battery life is around the same as other similar handhelds, which is quite good. This is especially true given the AYANEO 1S DS is powering a dual display.

## Navigating the Dual-Screen Software

With the built-in [AYANEO](https://droix.net/product-attribute/brands/ayaneo/ "AYANEO") software, you can navigate through various tabs at the bottom of the second screen. These tabs can be used to show current system stats, including TDP, fan speed, and resource usage. The second screen also allows you to change settings. This includes helpful shortcuts to Extreme, Balanced, and Power-saving modes.

### Quick Assistant and App Management

The Quick Assistant feature provides large icons for common functions. For instance, you can easily switch between desktop modes, take screenshots, toggle fullscreen, and minimize software. Furthermore, the “Application” section lets you add shortcuts to your frequently used apps.

### Smart Dual-Screen Functionality

Menu Application



Menu Monitor

A feature named “smart dual-screen” is designed to easily let you move currently open software into the bottom display of the AYANEO 1S DS. From here, you could open your browser, for example, and then move it down. The software should show apps moving in and out of the bottom screen.

However, for some reason, I couldn’t get the browser to display this way automatically. I had to manually move the windows to the bottom screen instead.

## How Does the AYANEO 1S DS Perform in Games?

As we know from many other HX 370-based compact gaming PC models, the processor is quite capable. It can run games at decent resolutions and graphics settings. Specifically, at 1080P, you can generally play games at medium to high settings and achieve 60 FPS.

Regarding the second display, you can use it for playing a video, following a walkthrough guide, or keeping Discord chat open. You could even do a bit of shopping at DROIX 😉

Realistically, however, you will probably have the second display switched off most of the time. Alternatively, you might just use it for showing performance stats.

## Emulation and Dual-Screen Potential

The HX 370 processor is also great for emulation. It can run systems up to the more recent handhelds with very few issues. Consequently, we can take full advantage of dual-screen handheld emulators, such as melonDS and [Azahar](https://azahar-emu.org/ "Azahar"), on the AYANEO 1S DS.

Azahar 3D Dual Screen emulator



melonDS Dual Screen emulator



Vita3K Emulator

These dual screen handheld emulators do require a bit of setting up. This is necessary to take advantage of both screens of this handheld gaming computer. The process involves changing some menu options to create a second window. This window must then be made to display on the second screen.

It should be noted that this setup is not quite perfect in all cases. You may require 3rd-party software to make the emulated second screen truly full screen.

## Final Thoughts: Our AYANEO 1S DS Review Verdict

The AYANEO 1S DS comes with some decent improvements over the original AYANEO DS model. If you were a fan of the original, then you will undoubtedly love the AYANEO 1S DS.

The new, larger second display makes things a great deal easier to use and view. Of course, the faster processor is always a welcome addition.

Tony Hawks Pro Skater

However, the main question is: do you really need a dual-screen handheld? If you spend a lot of time playing dual-screen emulators or frequently referring to game guides, then it’s a clear yes.

Otherwise, you will probably use the second screen for a few days and then ignore it once the novelty wears off. In this case, I would consider any other HX 370-based handheld as a viable alternative.
