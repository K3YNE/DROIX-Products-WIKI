## GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Overview

Next in our GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 review we take a closer look at the portable gaming PC. The [GPD WIN MAX 2 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-max-2-2024/) measures 8.9 x 6.2 x 0.9 inches (22.7 x 16 x 2.3 cm) and weighs 1005g.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Dimensions

The display is a 10.1 inch touchscreen with a default 1200P resolution, supporting up to 1600P. It’s a great sized screen for both work and gaming on this [handheld gaming PC](https://droix.net/product-category/gaming-handheld/handheld-gaming-pc/).

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Display

The lower half of the device contains a decent sized backlit keyboard that is great for typing on, I wrote this review with it and had no issues at all. The keys are low profile and require little effort to press.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Keyboard

Above the keys are the removable covers which slide into the back of the device. Underneath are your standard gaming controls with hall sensor analogues, D-Pad and gaming buttons. In the middle is a Microsoft Precision TouchPad. And on the hinge is a built-in camera for video conferencing.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Controls

On the left side are micro and full sized SD card slots. And on the right are two USB 3.2 Gen 1 ports.

The front has a power button with a built-in fingerprint sensor for fast and secure logging into Windows.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Back View

The back has triggers and shoulder buttons, and there are USB 4 and USB 3.2 Gen 2 ports. Next there is a full size HDMI port for output to a display, and an Oculink port for connection to eGPU docking stations such as the GPD G1. And last but not least there is a 3.5mm headphone port.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Bottom View

On the bottom are two additional macro buttons that can be configured in the supplied software. On either side are removable covers, one for m.2 2230 SSD expansion and the other for the optional 4G LTE module.

## System Benchmarks

We will now perform some system benchmarks and use the results to compare against the 2023 model as well as other devices with the 8840U CPU such as the [GPD WIN Mini 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-mini-2024/).

### PCMark

We start with PCMark which performs a series of your more day to day usage of a PC from web browsing to image and video editing.

PCMARK Benchmark Comparison

We got a score of 7,085 which takes the lead over the [GPD WIN 4 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-4-2024/) handheld gaming PC. It is also a considerable improvement over last year’s WIN MAX 2 model which is good to see.

### Cinebench

Cinebench tests the single and multi core performance of the processor.

Cinebench Benchmark Comparison

We got single and multi-core scores of 1,768 and 12,997 respectively. Again, it takes the lead in single core and falls just shy of the GPD Win 4 2024 mobile gaming PC in multi-core performance. There is a good improvement over the 2023 model.

### 3DMark

3DMARK tests the CPU and GPU working together for graphics related performance such as gaming and video encoding.

3DMARK Benchmark Comparison

In Time Spy, Night Raid and Fire Strike, we got scores of 3,114, 27,221 and 7,307. Not much difference to last year’s model, although the Win 4 ’24 is not much higher either apart from on Night Raid.

As this is a portable gaming computer as part of our GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 review we will benchmark some popular games. We test with a range of TDP from 5W to 28W and at 1080P and 720P resolutions to compare.

### Forza Horizon 5

We are running the Forza Horizon 5 benchmark on the lowest graphics settings.

Forza Horizon 5 Benchmark Comparison

At 1080P 28W we see a few frames difference in performance compared to the GPD Win 4 ’24, and a decent  improvement over the 2023 model by 30 FPS.

At 720P we do see near identical FPS, but there’s a noticeable performance drop when compared to the other models as we go down the TDPs.

### Shadow of the Tomb Raider

Shadow of the Tomb Raider Benchmark Comparison

Call of Duty Modern Warfare III Benchmark Comparison

Street Fighter 6 Benchmark Comparison

## Final Thoughts

Time to sum up our GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 review now. The [GPD WIN MAX 2 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-max-2-2024/) was the final of GPD handheld gaming computers to be updated and overall  it is a good improvement over last year’s model. We see improved performance when comparing the [7840U](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/ryzen-7-7840u/) and 8840U processors. Like the other models, perhaps not enough to warrant upgrading if you have the 2023 model, but if you are looking for a device that is ideal for work and gaming, the Win MAX 2 2024 is an excellent option.

GPD WIN MAX 2 2024 Little Kitty Big City

Gaming on the WIN MAX 2 series has always been a talking point. It’s not a mobile gaming PC you can freely hold, you will be resting it on your lap or on a table for example, or maybe using an external controller instead. But it is quite comfortable and easy to game on as well as do work with. If perhaps you are more of a gamer than using it for work, it is worth considering the [GPD Win Mini 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-mini-2024/) instead.

The 10.1 inch screen is great to work with and even better to game on. You will be playing the more demanding games at 1080P or even 720P, but for work you can happily crank the resolution up to 1600P and enjoy the full benefits of higher resolutions and larger screen than the [GPD WIN 4 2024](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-4-2024/) and GPD WIN Mini 2024 models.
