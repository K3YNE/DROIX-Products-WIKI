## GMKTec NucBox G5’s Technical Specifications

The [GMKTec NucBox G5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-g5/) is powered by an Intel [N97](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/n97/) processor, boasting 4 cores and 4 threads, with a maximum turbo frequency of 3.6 GHz and a TDP of just 12W. This efficient CPU is built on Intel’s 7nm process, ensuring energy efficiency and solid performance. The integrated Intel UHD Graphics, running at 1.2 GHz with 24 execution units, provides decent graphical capabilities for everyday tasks and light gaming.

The mini PC with Intel processor comes with 12GB of [LPDDR5](https://droix.net/product-attribute/memory-ram-technology/lpddr5/) RAM, clocked at 4800 MT/s, and a 512GB M.2 2242 SATA SSD, expandable up to 2TB. Connectivity options include [Bluetooth 4.2](https://droix.net/product-attribute/bluetooth/4-2/) and [Wi-Fi 5](https://droix.net/product-attribute/wi-fi/5/), ensuring reliable wireless communication. For video output, it supports dual 4K@60Hz displays via its two HDMI ports. Additionally, it features a total of 9 I/O ports, including three USB 3.2 Gen1 ports, a 3.5mm headphone jack, a micro SD slot, a Gigabit Ethernet port, and a USB-C port for power.

## Build and Design

The [GMKTec NucBox G5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-g5/) is remarkably compact, measuring just 72 x 72 x 44.5mm and weighing only 168 grams. Despite its small size, it includes a VESA mounting bracket for easy installation behind monitors or TVs, making it an excellent choice for space-constrained environments. The device’s design emphasizes portability without sacrificing performance, making it an ideal Intel small form factor PC.

GMKTec NucBox M5

Inside the NucBox G5, the 512GB SSD and 12GB of LPDDR5 RAM provide ample storage and memory for multitasking and data-intensive applications. The device is powered by a 12V 3A (36W) Type-C charger, further enhancing its portability and ease of use.

## Benchmark Results and Performance

In benchmarks, the [GMKTec NucBox G5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-g5/) with Intel 12th Gen exhibits solid performance for a device of its size. In Cinebench R23, the NucBox G5 maintained consistent core usage and temperatures around 89°C under load. The CPU, running at a stable 2.9 GHz, delivered impressive multi-core scores comparable to similar mini PCs with Alder Lake CPUs, such as the N100.

GMKTec NucBox M5 Intel 12th Gen

The GMKTec NucBox G5 also supports 4K@60Hz output from both HDMI ports, proving its capability for high-resolution displays. In terms of gaming, titles like “Forza Horizon 4” and “Tomb Raider 2013” ran smoothly at 720p low settings, showing that the NucBox G5 can handle light gaming and older titles efficiently. Emulation performance was also commendable, making it a versatile option for retro gaming enthusiasts.
