## Using the DroiX PM14 portable monitor

In this part of our DroiX PM14 review we will demonstrate the different connectivity methods. There are a number of ways you can connect your devices to the [DroiX PM14](https://droix.net/product/droix-pm14-portable-monitor/) portable monitor.

HDMI connection example

If you have a device with HDMI output you can use the supplied HDMI to mini HDMI cable for the video. Then use the supplied power supply and USB-A to Type-C cable to power the monitor.

USB C connection example

If you have a device that supports USB-C video output, then you can simply connect the supplied USB-C to USB-C cable to one of the ports on the right side and it will use that for both the video and power.

However if your device does not supply enough power or you want to conserve battery life on your laptop for example, you can also connect the supplied charger to the second USB-C port on the monitor.

### Touch screen model

For the touch screen model you can enable support for this by two ways. The first, if your device supports it, is via the USB-C to USB-C cable as shown earlier. With one cable you can have power, video and have touch screen support which is great.

Touch screen connection example

If your device does not have a Type-C port, you can use the included HDMI and the USB-A to Type-C data cable which will send the data to and from the monitor to your device.
