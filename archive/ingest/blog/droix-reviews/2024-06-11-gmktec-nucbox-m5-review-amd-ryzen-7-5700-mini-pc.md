## GMKTec NucBox M5 Overview

### Unmatched Performance and Graphics

At the heart of the [GMKTec NucBox M5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m5/) is the AMD [Ryzen 7 5700](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/ryzen-7-5700u/) processor, featuring 8 cores and 16 threads. This powerhouse CPU operates at a base clock speed of 1.8GHz, with the ability to boost up to 4.3GHz, ensuring smooth and efficient multitasking, gaming, and professional applications. The integrated [Radeon Graphics](https://droix.net/product-attribute/graphics-gpu-model/radeon-graphics/), with 8 cores running at 1900 MHz, delivers exceptional visual performance, making it suitable for everything from casual gaming to professional graphic design mini PC for home office. The NucBox M5 supports up to three displays simultaneously through its HDMI 2.0, [DisplayPort](https://droix.net/product-attribute/i-o-video/1x-displayport-1-4/), and Type-C ports, each capable of delivering 4K resolution at 60Hz.

### Ample Memory and Connectivity

The [GMKTec NucBox M5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m5/) is equipped with 16GB of DDR4 3200MHz RAM, which can be expanded up to 64GB to meet the demands of intensive applications. Its 512GB PCIe 3.0 M.2 2280 SSD offers rapid boot times and quick access to files, with support for storage expansion up to 4TB. Connectivity is another strong suit of this mini PC for business, featuring [Bluetooth 5.2](https://droix.net/product-attribute/bluetooth/5-2/), [WiFi 6E](https://droix.net/product-attribute/wi-fi/6e/), and a wide range of I/O ports including USB3.2 Gen2, USB2.0, HDMI 2.0, DisplayPort, Type-C, and dual 2.5G Ethernet ports, ensuring you have all the necessary connections for your peripherals and network needs.

## Benchmark Results and Performance

In our tests, the [GMKTec NucBox M5](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-m5/) demonstrated impressive performance across various benchmarks. In PC User Benchmark, it scored notably higher than several competitors, outperforming recent Intel i7 mini PCs in overall performance. Geekbench scores also highlighted its prowess, particularly in GPU performance where it excelled in OpenCL tasks, making it an excellent choice for graphics-intensive applications. When we pushed the TDP to 35W on this AMD compact computer, we observed significant gains in benchmark scores, but this came at the cost of increased heat and power consumption.

GMKTec NucBox M5 Breakdown

Gaming benchmarks further solidified the GMKTec NucBox M5’s standing as a capable mini PC for gaming and emulation. In ultrawide QHD resolution, it achieved around 47 FPS in Rocket League on performance settings and 30 FPS in Tekken 7 on medium settings. For less demanding games like League of Legends, it maintained a smooth 90-100 FPS at medium settings. Emulation tests were also promising, with the M5 handling PS2 and some PS3 games at playable speeds, and even running demanding Wii U titles like Mario Odyssey at 50 FPS. These results highlight the NucBox M5’s versatility, making it a strong contender in the mini AMD desktop category.
