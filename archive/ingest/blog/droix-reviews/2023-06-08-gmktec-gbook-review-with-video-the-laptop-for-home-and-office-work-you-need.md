## Overview of the GMKTec Gbook

We continue the GMKTec Gbook review with a look at its features.It measures around 12.3 x 8.5 x 0.4 inches and weighs 1.3kg.

Lifting up the lid reveals the 14” IPS display which looks great. It has a maximum resolution of 4K 60Hz which is something not seen often on smaller sized displays.

GMKTec Gbook Display

There is a camera in the bezel which can be physically blocked with a slider if privacy is a concern. The 720P image quality is pretty good and suitable for online meetings.

The lower half of the [GMKTec Gbook](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-gbook/) has your standard low profile backlit keyboard. I wrote this review on the keyboard and it is perfectly fine to use for extended typing sessions.

GMKTec Gbook Keyboard

Below is a touchpad for mouse input with clickable areas for controls. You will notice the small square in the top left area, that is the [fingerprint sensor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fingerprint_scanner) for securely logging into Windows on this Intel laptop.

GMKTec Gbook Left Side

On the left side of the Gbook there is the power port, USB Type-C, HDMI output, and one USB 3.2 port.

GMKTec Gbook Right Side

On the right side is a 3.5mm multi jack, two USB 3.2 ports and a micro SD card slot.

When the lid is opened the hinge design raises the laptop a little off your work surface. This is primarily to assist with good airflow for the cooling. It does make it a little more comfortable when typing as it rises towards the back of the keyboard.

## System Benchmarks

As always, as part of our [GMKTec Gbook](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-gbook/) review, we like to do some system benchmarks to see how well a device performs.

### PassMark

We got a PassMark score of 3611.2 which is around the average score. Breaking down the scores we can see that the processor got above the average with 12K. The 2D and 3D graphics tests are where this laptop for home office falls below average, but remember this is not a gaming laptop and is perfectly fine for any other task as a laptop for work.

PassMark Benchmark Result

The scores go back above the average for the Memory test, and the Storage test is in the very high percentile with nearly 25 thousand.

### PCMark

PCMark performs a series of tests on your more day to day usages from web browsing to working with large office documents. We got a score of 4986 with excellent scores, from 9472 and 6768, but lower for when working with image and video media.

PCMark Benchmark Result

### 3DMark

3DMark tests the performance of the CPU and GPU working together. We got a very respectable score of 1342 on the Time Spy benchmark, 3610 on Fire Storm and 12259 on Night Raid. It is definitely capable of some light gaming at the least.

3DMark Benchmark Result

### Cinebench

Cinebench is a CPU test which can test either single or multi core performance. On the multicore test we got a score of 5404. It is around the expected score for this processor.

Cinebench Benchmark Result

### CrystalDiskMark

We saw from the PC Mark results some great scores for the storage. A quick run of CrystalDiskMark shows read speeds of just under 3.5GB/s and write speeds of 3.2GB/s. Both are very high speed and you will notice the difference in large file transfers for example.

CrystalDiskMark Benchmark Result

## Gaming Performance

While this is far from being a gaming laptop, more of a laptop for home office, as part of this [GMKTec Gbook](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-gbook/) review we can’t resist trying them as it can run some games just fine. As a test, we ran the Forza Horizon benchmark at 720P on the Very Low graphics settings and we got a fairly impressive 37 FPS which is enough to keep the game playable locked to 30 FPS with some minor dips in performance now and again.

Forza Horizon 5 is playable with the right settings

Something a little simpler but equally as fun. Lower demanding games work great! You can run them at higher resolutions and/or graphics quality and get some decent results. The recently released Minecraft Legends is running well above 60 FPS, and the great Turtles Shredder’s Revenge runs perfectly.

So while it is not a gaming laptop, you can sneak in some light gaming after work hours 😉
