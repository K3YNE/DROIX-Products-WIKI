## BudPlus S3 Projector Overview

Next in our BudPlus S3 Projector review we take a closer look at the projector. The [BudPlus S3 projector](https://droix.net/product/budplus-s3-projector/) measures 7.16 x 6.7 x 7.7 inches (18.2 x 17.1 x 19.8 cm) and weighs 1.6Kg.

BudPlus S3 projector top view of touch controls

On the top of the projector we have touch sensitive controls for commonly used functions for menu navigation. You can of course use the included remote control if you prefer.

BudPlus S3 projector front view

On the front there is the projector lens, it is native 1920×1080 output at 30Hz with a brightness of 7500 lumens. It can display on a surface between 1 and 6 metres distance, providing up to a 300” projected screen. It can of course be ceiling mounted as well as used on a table or tripod for example. The hardware supports automatic and manual focus as well as automatic keystone correction.

BudPlus S3 projector side view

The left and right sides have high quality JBL harman stereo speakers which pack a decent punch. Not like those tinny speakers you usually get in projectors. There is also a hand strap if you wish to carry it.

BudPlus S3 projector back view

On the back are two USB ports which you can use for example to connect a memory stick and wireless keyboard and mouse. There is a 3.5mm headphone port and an AV in port which you can use with the supplied cables. And at the end is a HDMI input which you can connect from any device with HDMI output such as a PC, games console and even gaming handhelds.

BudPlus S3 projector bottom view

You can navigate the menus and use the pre-installed software like you would similar to an Android TV box or phone.

BudPlus S3 projector App Store

## Usage

We continue the BudPlus S3 Projector review with some usage examples. You can use the supplied media players to play your content on the [mini projector](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-projectors/). There is support for a wide variety of video formats from MPEG1 up to H.265 and audio such as MP3 through to FLAC. You can play these directly from your memory stick if you wish to. Or you could get more advanced and connect wirelessly to a NAS with compatible apps for example.

BudPlus S3 projector video example

If you have an external device, you can change the input to HDMI or AV. You can connect any compatible device such as games console, PC, handheld and display up to 1080P. For older hardware such as a VHS recorder or camcorder you can use the AV cable.

For more modern devices such as mobile phones, you can use the built in mirror cast and google cast over wifi. Simply follow the on screen steps to get connected and you can display content from your device.

BudPlus S3 projector retro gaming

It is possible to install your own apps. You can do this through the web browser or download the APKs to a USB drive and install from there. We installed [RetroArch](https://www.retroarch.com/) and set up a few emulators to see the performance. We saw very good performance for all the 8-bit and 16-bit systems. When we get to PlayStation 1 performance we start to see some performance issues on games. You can also get PSP running, but again you will see some performance issues. Overall it is very impressive for this CPU and a nice bonus to have if you are a retro gamer.

BudPlus S3 projector auto keystone

There are automatic and manual focus options which will sharpen the projected display. And if you have the [BudPlus S3 projector](https://droix.net/product/budplus-s3-projector/) at an angle, the automatic keystone will adjust when the angle is changed so that it will display correctly on the projected surface.

The built in JBL Harman speakers are very good quality, it’s impossible to reproduce audio quality over recorded video. Check out our BudPlus S3 projector review video above for a sample of the quality.

For the average house living room, in the UK especially, the speakers will definitely fill out your room and you will not require external speakers like with other projectors.

Unedited image showing the projector

We have limited space in the office, so we have been trying on the largest white wall we have for demonstration purposes. We do advise buying a decent quality projection screen for the best quality display. Our office blinds were open while recording so it is letting in some light, we recommend the room being as dark as possible for the best results.

## Final Thoughts

We sum up our BudPlus S3 Projector review with our final thoughts. The main highlights with the [BudPlus S3 projector](https://droix.net/product/budplus-s3-projector/) are that it is actual 1080P quality, not upscaled from lower resolutions. It requires as little as 1 metre to get a projected display, obviously 6 metres will give a larger display though. It has Android 9 which means you have a choice of apps to use, you can even sideload your own. The audio quality is great with the JBL Harman stereo speakers.
