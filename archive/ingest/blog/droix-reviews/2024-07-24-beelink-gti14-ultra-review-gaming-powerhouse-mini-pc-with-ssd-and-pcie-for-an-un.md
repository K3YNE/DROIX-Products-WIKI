## Mini PC Design and Build

Beelink GTi14 UItra

We start the Beelink GTi14 Ultra review with a closer look at the design and ports. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra features a compact yet slightly larger design compared to typical mini PC with Intel processor, measuring 158 x 158 x 56mm (6.2″ x 6.2″ x 2.2″). Its aluminum housing gives it a premium feel and durability, with a design reminiscent of the Mac Mini. The device weighs more than standard [mini PCs](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-pcs/) due to its integrated 145W power supply, eliminating the need for an external adapter. On the front panel, users will find a power button with an integrated fingerprint sensor, a status LED light, and an SD card reader.

Beelink GTi14 Ultra IO

The GTi14 Ultra boasts an impressive array of ports, including one Thunderbolt 4 (40 Gbps), one USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-C (10 Gbps), five USB 3.2 Gen 2 Type-A (10 Gbps) ports, HDMI 2.1, DisplayPort 1.4a, two 3.5mm audio jacks, and two 2.5 GbE Ethernet. These are essential for many small form factor PCs, including those from [Beelink](https://droix.net/product-attribute/brands/beelink/). Uniquely, the bottom of the device houses a PCIe x8 interface hidden behind a small door, allowing for direct connection to an external graphics card dock This feature sets the GTi14 Ultra apart from most mini PCs, offering enhanced graphics capabilities without compromising its compact form factor

## Hardware Specifications

Beelink GTi14 Ultra CPU

We continue our Beelink GTi14 Ultra review with an inspection of the hardware inside. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra boasts impressive hardware specifications, offering high performance in a compact form factor. It features Intel’s latest Meteor Lake processors, with options up to the Intel [Core Ultra 9 185H](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/core-ultra-9-185h/). The integrated GPU is Intel Arc Graphics, providing improved graphics performance over previous generations. For memory, the GTi14 Ultra supports dual-channel [DDR5](https://droix.net/product-attribute/memory-ram-technology/ddr5-so-dimm/) 5600MHz RAM, expandable up to 96GB. Storage options include dual M.2 2280 PCIe 4.0 SSD slots, supporting up to 8TB of fast internal storage. Communication capabilities are robust, with an Intel BE200 wireless card supporting WiFi 7 and Bluetooth 5.4. The device also features two 2.5 GbE Ethernet ports for high-speed wired connections.

Power consumption is relatively efficient, with the GTi14 Ultra using around 30W when idle on the desktop and maxing out at a little over 80W under heavy CPU and GPU load during gaming. The integrated 145W power supply unit ensures stable operation at 65W TDP. Notably, the GTi14 Ultra mini PC for home office operates quietly, with a near-silent 32dB noise level, thanks to its efficient bottom air intake cooling system.

## System Benchmark Performance Analysis

Beelink GTi14 Ultra benchmarks

As part of our Beelink GTi14 Ultra review we performed a range of benchmarks. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra demonstrates impressive performance across various benchmark tests. In Geekbench, it achieved a single-core score of 2,270 and a multi-core score of 11,834, showcasing strong CPU performance. The integrated Intel Arc GPU scored 37,460 in Geekbench’s GPU benchmark, indicating very good performance for an integrated solution.

In Furmark, a GPU stress test, the GTi14 Ultra scored 1,920, though GPU temperature data was unavailable. 3DMark benchmarks further highlight the system’s capabilities, with scores of 12,862 in Sky Diver, 3,283 in Fire Strike, and 1,437 in Time Spy. These results suggest the GTi14 Ultra can handle demanding tasks and even gaming, especially when paired with an external GPU through its PCIe x8 interface. The benchmarks were conducted with surprisingly quiet fan operation, indicating efficient thermal management

## Internal and External GPU Gaming Performance Benchmarks

Beelink GTi14 Ultra features

And as this mini PC can game, we will as part of the Beelink GTi14 Ultra review, run some more benchmarks. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra demonstrates impressive gaming capabilities, especially when paired with an external GPU. Benchmark tests reveal significant performance improvements when utilizing the PCIe x8 interface for an external graphics card. With the integrated Intel Arc Graphics, the GTi14 Ultra achieves respectable frame rates in less demanding titles.

In Counterstrike, with graphics settings at Very High, the system manages over 60 fps using just the integrated GPU. However, the true gaming potential of the GTi14 Ultra is unlocked when an external GPU is connected, particularly for playing 4K video games. Using a XFX Radeon RX 6600 GPU through the PCIe x8 interface, gaming performance sees a dramatic boost:

* Counterstrike: Frame rates jump to over 200 fps at Very High settings, more than tripling the performance of the integrated GPU.
* [Doom Eternal](https://bethesda.net/en/game/doom): With Ultra Nightmare graphics settings and Ray Tracing disabled, the system achieves over 150 fps, approximately 2.5 times better than the integrated GPU performance.

These results highlight the GTi14 Ultra’s versatility as a gaming platform. The ability to switch between power-efficient integrated graphics for everyday tasks and high-performance external GPUs for gaming sessions makes it an attractive option for users seeking a compact yet capable gaming setup. It’s worth noting that the external GPU setup not only improves frame rates but also enhances overall system performance. Users report faster load times and a cooler-running system when using an external GPU, with the PC’s fan running noticeably quieter during gaming sessions.

While these benchmarks showcase impressive performance, it’s important to consider that actual gaming experiences may vary depending on the specific external GPU used and the games being played. Additionally, the availability and pricing of Beelink’s official external GPU dock solution may impact the overall value proposition for gaming enthusiasts.

## AI Capabilities and Applications

A new part to our Beelink GTi14 Ultra review is a look at the AI capabilities. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra incorporates advanced AI features in this mini PC for business, leveraging the capabilities of Intel’s latest Meteor Lake processors. These processors integrate a dedicated Neural Processing Unit (NPU) alongside the CPU and GPU, enabling efficient AI-powered tasks.

Beelink GTi14 Ultra AI

The [mini PC’s](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-pcs/) AI capabilities extend to voice interaction, featuring a built-in microphone powered by an AI chip that supports smart audio pickup. This technology effectively separates voice commands from background noise, allowing for 360° barrier-free voice recognition within a 5-meter range. Practical applications of these AI features found in the Intel compact computer include enhanced video conferencing experiences, where users can conduct meetings without leaving their seats. The AI-powered audio processing ensures clear voice capture even in noisy environments.

Additionally, the NPU can accelerate local AI computing tasks, potentially improving performance in applications such as image and video processing, natural language processing, and machine learning model inference. These features make the GTi14 Ultra suitable for professionals working with AI-enhanced productivity tools, content creators utilizing AI-powered editing software, and users interested in exploring AI applications without relying solely on cloud-based solutions.

## Comparison with Previous Models

The Beelink GTi14 Ultra represents a significant leap forward compared to previous Beelink models, particularly in its innovative approach to external graphics support. Unlike earlier mini PCs that relied on Thunderbolt or [OCuLink](https://droix.net/blogs/oculink-transforming-graphics-card-connectivity/) ports for external GPUs, the GTi14 Ultra features a PCIe x8 interface directly on the device’s bottom, allowing for a more efficient connection to desktop graphics cards .

This design, coupled with support for up to Intel Core Ultra 9 185H processors and 96GB of RAM, positions the GTi14 Ultra as a more powerful and versatile option than its predecessors Additionally, the integrated 145W power supply eliminates the need for an external power adapter, streamlining the setup compared to earlier models The inclusion of WiFi 7 and Bluetooth 5.4 support also puts the GTi14 Ultra mini Intel desktop ahead of previous generations in terms of connectivity options

## Final Verdict on the Beelink GTi14 Ultra review

Time to sum up our Beelink GTi14 Ultra review. The Beelink GTi14 Ultra represents a significant leap forward in mini PC design, offering a compelling blend of power, versatility, and innovation. Its standout feature is undoubtedly the PCIe x8 interface, which allows for the connection of an external GPU, potentially transforming this compact device into a capable gaming or workstation setup. The integration of Intel’s latest Meteor Lake processors, including AI capabilities and improved integrated graphics, positions the GTi14 Ultra as a formidable contender in the [mini PC](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-pcs/) market.

While the device impresses with its performance and feature set, including near-silent operation and a built-in power supply, it’s not without its drawbacks. The deeply recessed PCIe slot may limit compatibility with standard GPU risers, and the constant power draw even when shut down is a concern for energy-conscious users. Despite these minor issues, the GTi14 Ultra’s combination of powerful hardware, expandability, and innovative design makes it an attractive option for users seeking a versatile and compact computing solution that can adapt to various use cases, from everyday tasks to more demanding applications
