## Triple Aero 15.6″ ProMax Monitor Overview

When folded, the monitor measures approximately 14.1 x 16.5 x 1.1 inches and weighs 3.2kg (7.0 lbs). The displays are housed within a robust aluminum alloy frame, featuring high-quality hinges that allow for bidirectional folding. This design enables various display positions, which we’ll explore further.

Triple Aero 156 Pro Max Folded

The frame offers three levels of height adjustment. However, we noted that the third (lowest) setting felt somewhat insecure and prone to slipping out of its groove. Given the frame’s weight, stability is crucial to prevent accidental collapses and we do not suggest using the third grove.

Three height adjustments on the stand

The three 15.6” 1080P 60Hz LED displays feature a matte finish, effectively reducing glare. While 15.6” might seem small for a single monitor, the combined width of three side-by-side displays spans an impressive 43.7 inches when fully extended.

Triple Aero 156 Pro Max Unfolded

Along the bottom edge of the central monitor, you’ll find four buttons for the on-screen display (OSD), two USB-C ports (one for charging, one for video input), and three mini HDMI ports, allowing for up to three separate device connections.

Triple Aero 156 Pro Max OSD Buttons USB C Mini HDMI Ports

The two built-in speakers provide passable audio quality. Our “crab rave” audio test, as expected, yielded no bass and an average mix of lows and mids. They function adequately for general use, but external speakers would be advisable for an enhanced experience when watching films or playing games.

Back of the central monitor

For secure transport, two metal tabs keep the folded monitors closed, and the frame itself doubles as a convenient carrying handle.

## The OSD

The OSD is navigated using four buttons located on the underside of the central monitor. The first button accesses the menu and cycles through monitors, while the second and third serve as Up/Down controls (also shortcuts for volume and brightness). The fourth button selects options.

The On Screen Display menu

The OSD menus include standard settings like brightness, contrast, sharpness, color temperature, and language.

The OSD settings

A notable feature is the ability to independently control settings for each monitor via the OSD, even with multiple devices connected. We successfully tested this with three different devices. However, we discovered that audio output is limited to the first monitor; there was no option to route audio from devices connected to the second or third monitors.

## Exploring the monitor setups

A triple monitor setup significantly enhances desktop productivity. Connectivity is flexible: a single USB Type-C output can instantly enable a triple monitor setup, or you can use individual HDMI or DisplayPort (with adapter) outputs from your PC to connect to the mini HDMI ports.

Triple monitor with one USB C cable

The monitor can fold back into a “triangle” orientation, ideal for collaborative settings where displays can be mirrored for shared viewing around a table. A thoughtful magnetic closure keeps the two adjustable monitors securely connected in this configuration.

The triangle orientation for screen sharing with others



Triangle orientation side view

With three mini HDMI ports, you can also connect and use three different devices independently. This versatility could cater to diverse needs, such as one person watching a movie, another browsing the web, and a third playing a game console such as the Nintendo Switch 2 simultaneously. While potentially distracting, this setup is particularly effective in the triangle orientation. Other practical uses include gaming on one screen while using the others for Discord or stream monitoring.

Connect up to three different devices

We tried a variety of devices including PC & MacBook laptops, [mini PCs](https://droix.net/product-category/mini-pcs/), handheld gaming PC’s such as the [GPD WIN MAX 2 2025](https://droix.net/product/gpd-win-max-2-2025/), game consoles including Nintendo Switch 2 & PS5 and iPhone 15 phone. All displayed without any issues on their respective connectivity methods.

## Final Thoughts

The [Triple Aero 15.6″ ProMax Monitor](https://aura-displays.com/products/triple-aero-15-6-pro-max) is an intriguing concept that addresses several common multi-monitor challenges. For a permanent home or office setup, it eliminates the need for bulky monitor arms, offering a clean, integrated solution. Its connectivity is a major advantage; unlike many [portable monitors](https://droix.net/product-category/portable-monitors/) with side-mounted ports that hinder close placement, the Triple Aero’s bottom-edge ports ensure tidy cable management and allow for seamless, aesthetically pleasing monitor alignment.

A close up of the central monitor



A close up of the hinges

The capability to connect up to three different devices is a significant plus, especially for multi-user environments or collaborative tasks in the triangle orientation. While portability wasn’t the primary focus of our initial assessment, the folding design and integrated handle do make it a viable portable solution. At 3.2kg, it carries some weight, but this is understandable given it encompasses three displays and a sturdy frame, making it far more convenient than transporting three individual monitors with separate stands and power supplies. Essentially anything that connect via USB-C video output or to (mini) HDMI should work fine but you may need convertors for some.

Work and a bit of gaming in between

The [Triple Aero 15.6″ ProMax Monitor](https://aura-displays.com/products/triple-aero-15-6-pro-max) offers a unique and versatile solution for expanding screen real estate, proving to be a compelling option for both static desk setups and on-the-go productivity; despite minor issues with OSD scaling and the lowest height adjustment, its sturdy aluminum build, integrated cable management, and ability to connect up to three separate devices make it a highly functional and aesthetically pleasing triple-display system, especially given its relatively portable design for a multi-monitor setup.
