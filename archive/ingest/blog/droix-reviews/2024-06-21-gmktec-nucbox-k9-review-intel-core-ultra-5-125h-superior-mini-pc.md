## GMKTec NucBox K9’s Technical Specifications

The [GMKTec NucBox K9](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-k9/) is powered by Intel’s cutting-edge [Core Ultra 125H](https://droix.net/product-attribute/processor-cpu-model/core-ultra-5-125h/) processor, featuring 14 cores and 18 threads, built on 4nm technology with a base clock of 3.6GHz and turbo boost up to 4.5GHz. Accompanying this CPU is the [Intel Arc](https://droix.net/product-attribute/graphics-gpu-model/intel-arc-graphics/) graphics processor clocked at 2.2GHz, delivering robust graphical performance. It ships with 32GB of [DDR5](https://droix.net/product-attribute/memory-ram-technology/ddr5-so-dimm/) 5600MHz RAM across two SO-DIMM slots, expandable up to an impressive 96GB, ensuring smooth multitasking and application performance.

GMKTec NucBox K9 Spec

Storage capabilities are enhanced with a swift 1TB Lexar NVMe PCIe Gen 4 SSD, offering read speeds up to 4.5GB/s and write speeds up to 4GB/s. The NucBox K9 also supports a secondary M.2 slot, accommodating up to 4TB PCIe 4.0 drives, providing ample space for data storage and quick access. Connectivity options are extensive with [Bluetooth 5.2](https://droix.net/product-attribute/bluetooth/5-2/), WiFi 6, and a comprehensive array of ports including USB-A 3.2 Gen2, HDMI 2.0, DisplayPort, and Type-C USB4.0, facilitating seamless connections to peripherals and external displays.

Designed for diverse applications ranging from server use to home entertainment and mobile offices, the [GMKTec NucBox K9](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-k9/) excels in scenarios requiring high performance and versatility. Whether it’s for business presentations, lightweight gaming, or security monitoring, this Intel small form factor PC offers reliability and efficiency in a compact form factor. Ideal for both professional and personal use, the NucBox K9 stands out with its powerful processing capabilities, expansive memory, and robust connectivity options.

## Build and Design

The [GMKTec NucBox K9](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-k9/) boasts a well-designed and compact form factor, measuring approximately 12 x 12 cm with a height of 5 cm and weighing 525 grams. The case is made of light gray plastic with black grilles for airflow. The front I/O includes two USB 3.2 Type-A ports, a USB-C Thunderbolt 4 port with DisplayPort support, and a 3.5mm audio jack. The rear features two additional USB 3.2 Type-A ports, DisplayPort 1.4, HDMI 2.0, and dual 2.5GB Ethernet ports.

GMKTec NucBox K9 Design Build

Opening the K9 is straightforward, with a top lid granting access to the SSD, RAM, and fan. Removing four screws provides access to the second M.2 slot and the RAM for easy upgrades. This makes the NucBox K9 not only powerful but also user-friendly for those who may need to upgrade their mini Intel desktop over time.

## Benchmark Results and Performance

In benchmark tests, the [GMKTec NucBox K9](https://droix.net/product/gmktec-nucbox-k9/) demonstrated impressive performance. In PCMark 10, it achieved a high score of 6,812 points, indicating strong capabilities in everyday tasks and heavy computing. The Cinebench R23 multi-core score was 12,938, and the single-core score was 1,729, showcasing its robust CPU performance.

GMKTec NucBox K9 Processor

In 3DMark Fire Strike, the K9 scored 6,590 points with a graphics score of 7,056 and a physics score of 2,328. In 3DMark Time Spy, it achieved a total score of 3,211, with a graphics score of 2,886 and a CPU score of 8,890. Geekbench 6 results showed 2,283 for single-core and 11,573 for multi-core performance. These scores highlight the NucBox K9’s ability to handle demanding applications and tasks effectively, making it an ideal Intel compact computer.
