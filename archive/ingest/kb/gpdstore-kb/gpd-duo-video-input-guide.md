One of the great features of the [GPD Duo](https://gpdstore.net/laptops/gpd-duo/) is that the second (top) display can also be used like a [portable monitor](https://gpdstore.net/category/portable-monitors/), you can connect many devices with USB-C video output. It may be possible to use a HDMI to USB-C video convertor, we have not tried this.

## How to use the GPD Duo Video Input

USB-C and Brightness Controls

On the left side of the top display is a USB-C port which you can connect compatible devices to (see the compatibility list below).

### USB-C to USB-C Cable

We recommend the [DroiX USB 4 Type-C cable](https://gpdstore.net/accessories/usb-4-0-type-c-cable/) for best compatibility. Connect one side of the USB-C cable to GPD Duo’s USB-C port and the other to the USB-C port on your device.

Connect the USB-C Cable



Connect the USB-C cable to your device

After a few moments, the display should switch from the GPD Duo’s Window desktop to whatever is showing on your connected device.

After a few moments the display will show the connected device

You can use the brightness Up/Down buttons just below the USB-C connector on the GPD Duo to adjust the top displays brightness. Other controls such as the resolution etc. can be controlled from the connected device.

## GPD Duo Video Input Tips

* You can use the video input while the [GPD Duo](https://gpdstore.net/laptops/gpd-duo/) is switched on and in Windows for example. The top display will switch from Windows desktop to the device you have connected automatically.
* If you want to save some battery power and not have the GPD Duo switched on, you can also connect a device while the Duo is powered off. The top display will switch on and display from the connected device.
* No audio from the connected device will play on the GPD Duo speakers. The speakers are only for the Duo’s audio and not video input.
